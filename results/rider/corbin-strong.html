§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Corbin Strong's 2026 was a season of consistent, front-of-race baroudeur work for the NSN Cycling ProTeam, the New Zealand puncheur-sprinter quietly stacking placings across the spring without quite breaking through to a headline win. His punchy versatility kept him in the mix on both hilly and bunch-sprint days, and the Giro d'Italia gave him a Grand Tour stage to show it: Strong was repeatedly active near the front, taking 5th on the fast Stage 18 finish into Pieve di Soligo as the sprint and breakaway specialists fought it out. That kind of top-five showing typified his Giro — a rider always present in the finale, hunting the right move and contesting the reduced-group and bunch finishes even against deeper-resourced WorldTour squads. As one of NSN's most reliable cards, he banked valuable results and exposure across the race. With the Giro heading to its Rome conclusion on 31 May, Strong's steady, opportunistic arc set him up to keep chasing that elusive marquee win in the season's second half.
