§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Romain Grégoire built his 2026 around the late-winter French one-day races and the Ardennes Classics, the terrain that best suits the young Groupama-FDJ puncheur-climber. He opened with a form-building Ruta del Sol, taking 3rd on Stage 5 and 5th overall, then 2nd at the Trofeo Laigueglia, where he made the decisive split on the Colla Micheri but was outnumbered by Bahrain when Santiago Buitrago soloed clear, before landing his headline win at the Faun Drôme Classic, where he attacked in a two-up move with Matteo Jorgenson and held the Visma rider off in an uphill sprint. Through the cobbled block he rode mostly in support, his profile pointing instead to the hilly Classics, and he arrived at Brabantse Pijl in good shape: he animated the finale, getting into the decisive late lead group on the Overijse circuit before it was caught on the final S-bend, then took 4th in the reduced-group sprint behind Anders Foldager. That aggressive ride set up an Ardennes week in which he featured among the favourites for Amstel, Flèche Wallonne and Liège.

Trofeo Laigueglia
LEADER P2 +0:25 · Groupama - FDJ United

Grégoire confirmed his strong early-season form on the punchy Ligurian finale. Mar 1, 2026
Faun Drôme Classic
LEADER P1 +0:00 · Groupama - FDJ United

Grégoire rode a patient, opportunistic race. Rather than forcing the issue on the early walls, he waited for the decisive selection, then was the only rider able to follow Jorgenson's acceleration on the Mur d'Allex with about 14 km to go. He shared the work in the two-man move to hold off the chase, sat on Jorgenson's wheel through the final climb and into the last kilometre, then produced the sharper finish to take the uphill sprint. A win defined by timing and positioning, and an early-season statement from the 23-year-old.
