Tour de France — Stage 15: Champagnole → Plateau de Solaison
GRAND TOUR
STAGE HUNTER — · Pinarello Q36.5 Pro Cycling Team

Pidcock was the driving force of the breakaway, setting the tempo on the Col de la Croisette and briefly leading the race with around 9km to go on the Plateau de Solaison. Tour de France — Stage 14: Mulhouse → Le Markstein
GRAND TOUR
STAGE HUNTER — · Pinarello Q36.5 Pro Cycling Team

Pidcock was present in the large early breakaway that formed on the Grand Ballon, but the group was caught by the charging UAE-led peloton before the Col du Haag. On the final climb, he was unable to follow the GC pace and finished deep in the chasing groups. His GC deficit grew from +4:15 to +7:59 — a loss of 3:44 to Pogacar on the day — and he fell from 4th to 9th overall. Stage 13's spectacular gain was significantly erased on the first true mountain day. Pidcock now trails the main GC group by nearly 2:30 in an effort to re-enter the top five.

  • 38 kmIn the large early break that forms over the Grand Ballon
  • 130 kmBreak caught; Pidcock's group absorbed by the peloton

Jul 17, 2026
Tour de France — Stage 13: Dole → Belfort
GRAND TOUR
LEADER P3 +0:02 · Pinarello Q36.5 Pro Cycling Team

Pidcock delivered one of the defining rides of his Tour de France campaign on Stage 13, jumping from 10th overall at +11:49 to 4th at +4:15 — a gain of 7 minutes 34 seconds on the peloton. His Pinarello-Q36.5 team drove the pace of the big breakaway group for much of the day, and on the Ballon d'Alsace climb Pidcock was the aggressor: he crested the summit first and pushed hard on the descent. He could not claw back Schmid and Tejada in the run-in to Belfort, but third place at +0:02 meant a massive haul. With GC rivals Vingegaard, Evenepoel, Ayuso and Pogacar all in the peloton 7:32 behind, Pidcock is now a genuine top-four contender heading into the Alps.

  • 174 kmCrests the Ballon d'Alsace first, drops Wellens with a sharp acceleration
  • 190 kmChase group cannot close the Schmid-Tejada gap with 15km to go
  • 206 kmFinishes third, moves from 10th (+11:49) to 4th (+4:15) in GC

Tour de France — Stage 9: Malemort → Ussel
GRAND TOUR
STAGE HUNTER P3 s.t. · Pinarello-Q36.5 Pro Cycling Team

Third place is an excellent result for Pidcock, made more impressive by a mid-race mechanical that threatened to eliminate him from the leading group on Mont Bessou. He chased back after the brief setback and rejoined the four-man front group in time for the final sprint. In the Ussel uphill finish he could not match Van der Poel's explosive speed but comfortably held third. For Pinarello-Q36.5, it is the team's best Tour result of 2026 — a podium that shows Pidcock is back near his best.

  • 140 kmMechanical on Mont Bessou; temporarily dropped from the lead group
