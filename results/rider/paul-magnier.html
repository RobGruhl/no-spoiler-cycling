§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Paul Magnier's 2026 was a star-is-born season, the year the young French sprinter announced himself at the very top of the WorldTour. He set the early-season template at the Volta ao Algarve, winning Stage 1 from a star-studded sprint, and arrived at the Giro d'Italia as Soudal Quick-Step's lead fast man with everything to prove. He delivered immediately, powering to victory on the chaotic, crash-marred opening stage in Burgas to claim both his first Grand Tour win and the maglia rosa, then backing it up on Stage 3 in Sofia to close out the Bulgarian Grande Partenza. His hat-trick arrived on Stage 18 in Pieve di Soligo, a third bunch-sprint win that confirmed his dominance over rivals like Jonathan Milan in the flat finales and kept him locked in the duel for the maglia ciclamino against breakaway king Jhonatan Narváez. Three stage wins in his maiden Giro made Magnier the breakout sprinter of the season. With the race continuing to its Rome finish on 31 May, the 2026 Giro stands as his launchpad toward the sport's biggest sprint stages.

May 28, 2026
Giro d'Italia — Stage 18: Fai della Paganella → Pieve di Soligo
GRAND TOUR
STAGE HUNTER P1 · Soudal Quick-Step

Magnier capped a stunning Giro with his third stage victory, completing a sprint hat-trick. The young Frenchman survived the steep Muro di Ca' del Poggio inside the final 10 km, staying near the front as the climb failed to fragment the bunch decisively. With the escapees caught just before the flamme rouge, his team set up the finale and Jasper Stuyven delivered a perfect lead-out. Magnier launched and powered clear convincingly, holding off Edoardo Zambanini and Jonathan Milan. The win also restored the maglia ciclamino to his shoulders, reclaiming the points lead from Jhonatan Narváez. It is the headline performance of his race — three wins from a single Grand Tour confirming his arrival among the sport's elite fast men.

  • Survived the Muro di Ca' del Poggio inside the final 10 km as the climb failed to split the sprinters out
  • Received a textbook lead-out from Jasper Stuyven in the closing kilometres
  • Won the bunch sprint convincingly for his third stage win, also reclaiming the ciclamino points jersey
