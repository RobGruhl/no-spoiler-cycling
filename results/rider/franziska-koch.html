§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Franziska Koch spent the 2026 cobbled spring as a luxury domestique for Demi Vollering at FDJ United-SUEZ — and then stole the biggest day of the campaign for herself. The German national champion's season turned at Paris-Roubaix Femmes on 12 April, where she was the only non-Visma rider to make the four-up move Pauline Ferrand-Prévot launched at Mons-en-Pévèle with 46 km to go. Left one-against-two once Blanka Vas was dropped, Koch refused to be controlled: strongest on the cobbles, repeatedly forcing Ferrand-Prévot to chase, then ice-cold in the velodrome where she held her line to beat Marianne Vos in a head-to-head sprint. It was the standout victory of her career and her first Monument, recasting a rider known for selfless work into a Classics winner in her own right — and capping a banner week for FDJ that also included the women's Tour of Flanders.

Apr 12, 2026
Paris-Roubaix Femmes
MONUMENT
LEADER P1 3h 30m 16s · FDJ United - SUEZ

The ride of her career. The German national champion was the only rider outside Visma to make the four-up move that Ferrand-Prévot launched at Mons-en-Pévèle, and once Vas was dropped at 23 km she stood alone against the two Visma riders. Rather than wait to be controlled, she attacked the situation — strongest on the cobbles, dropping Vas, repeatedly forcing Ferrand-Prévot to chase back, and going again inside the final 5 km. On the velodrome she refused the cat-and-mouse, held her line in a side-by-side sprint against Vos and edged ahead at the line. A spring spent supporting Demi Vollering ended with Koch a Monument winner in her own right.
