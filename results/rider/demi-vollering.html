§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Demi Vollering's first season at FDJ United-SUEZ became one of the great campaigns of modern women's racing. She opened in mid-February by winning the Setmana Ciclista Valenciana wire to wire — a 20 km solo over the Alt de Barx on Stage 1 took the jersey, and after the GC-deciding queen stage was cancelled for wind she sealed it with a second win in the Stage 4 group sprint in Valencia. From there she was a constant presence across the spring classics, in the mix at the Ardennes and the cobbled and hilly one-days where she repeatedly figured on or near the podium. The headline came at the Giro d'Italia Women, where she overturned a near-minute deficit on the final day to win the overall and complete the women's Grand Tour Triple Crown across the Tour de France Femmes, Vuelta and Giro. A relentless, front-of-race rider all year, Vollering combined GC dominance in stage races with classics aggression, and the Valenciana double was the early statement that set the tone for the season that followed.

Apr 26, 2026
Liège-Bastogne-Liège Femmes
MONUMENT
LEADER P1 winner · FDJ United - SUEZ

Vollering produced the defining ride of the women's spring. After FDJ massed at the front, she attacked on the Côte de la Redoute about 34-35 km out and dropped everyone instantly, cresting around 10 seconds clear and doubling the gap on the climb to Cornémont. She rode a measured 35 km solo, topping the Roche-aux-Faucons roughly 1:10 ahead and extending to win by 1:29. It was her record third Liège-Bastogne-Liège, an Ardennes double with Flèche Wallonne, and her fourth one-day victory of a season that also delivered Omloop Het Nieuwsblad and the Tour of Flanders.

  • 34 kmAttacked on the Côte de la Redoute and immediately dropped all rivals
  • 30 kmDoubled her lead on the rise to Cornémont
  • 13 kmCrested the Roche-aux-Faucons about 1:10 clear of the chase
Apr 22, 2026
La Flèche Wallonne Femmes
MONUMENT
LEADER P1 winner · FDJ United - SUEZ

Vollering won the Mur de Huy the hard way. Rather than wait for the traditional late kick, she moved to the front from the foot of the final climb and steadily wound up the pace, shredding the front group of around twenty. On the steepest pitch she opened a ten-metre gap and, although Pieterse's late charge brought the defending champion back onto her wheel, Vollering held on for victory — same time but never headed. It was her second Flèche Wallonne after 2023 and the first half of an Ardennes double sealed four days later with a record third Liège-Bastogne-Liège.

  • 1 kmTook the front from the foot of the final Mur de Huy and lifted the pace early
  • 0.5 kmAccelerated on the steepest section to open a decisive ten-metre gap
