§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Lorena Wiebes remained the benchmark sprinter of the 2026 women's spring, even on days the climbs denied her. As defending Milano-Sanremo Donne champion she was briefly distanced on the Cipressa and caught behind the decisive Poggio split, then led the chasing peloton home for sixth at +0:09 — the best of the rest behind a five-rider break that included her own teammate and eventual winner Lotte Kopecky. She found her own wins too — taking the IXINA Leeuw-Oetingen for a third time, this time by attacking over the Zavelberg rather than waiting for the bunch, and adding the Copenhagen Sprint Women — and was a fixture in the cobbled-classics finales, taking sixth at Paris-Roubaix Femmes (+2:20), a spring that confirmed her enduring status as the fastest finisher whenever the race stayed together.

LEADER P1 3h 31m 21s · Team SD Worx - Protime

A masterclass that doubled as a statement. Rather than wait for a passive sprint on a finale built to dislodge her, Wiebes attacked on the final ascent of the Kemmelberg and shed all but four rivals, taking the climbing into her own hands.
