§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Isaac del Toro's spring read like the plan for a UAE Team Emirates protected card with Pogačar skipping the Pogačar-skip races: home win at UAE Tour, Strade Bianche podium third behind Pogačar and Seixas, leader's jersey and queen-stage win at Tirreno-Adriatico. The trajectory was the cleanest run UAE has had on a non-Pogačar GC card in three seasons. And then it stopped — a crash on Stage 3 of Itzulia Basque Country ended the spring and turned a season-building April into a recovery one. The post-crash diagnosis was a right thigh muscle tear and abrasions, and as of mid-May he remained unable to race. Amstel, Flèche, Liège — all on the pre-injury programme, all forfeited.

The broader UAE injury picture by mid-May 2026 was a team crisis. Vine broken-elbow-and-concussion in the Giro Stage 2 crash, Soler fractured pelvis, Adam Yates concussion in the same incident, Del Toro still ongoing, Baroncini joining the absentee list — at least five UAE riders unable to race at once. Del Toro's recovery is one piece of a deeper UAE problem; his return date remained publicly undefined at compilation time.

Tour de France — Stage 10: Aurillac → Le Lioran
GRAND TOUR
LEADER P8 +1:31 · UAE Team Emirates XRG

A stage to forget for the young Mexican, the most significant GC reversal of the day. Del Toro had been third overall heading into Stage 10 but cracked badly in the final mountain section, finishing 1:31 behind Pogačar. Combined with the stage bonus loss, he surrendered 1:41 to the yellow jersey in one afternoon, plummeting from third to seventh overall at +5:08. For a rider who had looked secure in the top three, it is a harsh lesson in Tour de France mountain racing; how he responds in the Alps will define his 2026 campaign.

Tour de France — Stage 6: Pau → Gavarnie-Gèdre
GRAND TOUR
LEADER P3 +2:57 · UAE Team Emirates XRG

Del Toro is UAE's key engine on the Tourmalet, moving to the front of the group inside the final 5 km as part of the plan to isolate Pogačar's rivals. The 22-year-old can hold the pace himself until Pogačar accelerates — at which point only Vingegaard briefly follows before del Toro is dropped. He outsprints the chase group (Evenepoel, Seixas, Lipowitz, Ayuso, Skjelmose, Martinez) on the Gavarnie finish ramp for third place, earning bonus seconds that lift him from 8th to 3rd in GC at +3:27. A major step forward in his debut Tour de France.

Tour de France — Stage 2: Tarragona → Barcelona
GRAND TOUR
LEADER P1 · UAE Team Emirates XRG

Del Toro delivers one of the stage's most dramatic arcs: he suffers a mechanical issue near km 108, endures a roadside wait for a replacement bike due to a radio miscommunication, and still re-enters the decisive group in time for the Montjuïc finale. He reads the final ascent perfectly — chasing down Skjelmose's acceleration before counterattacking through a corner with speed no one can match. Pogačar tucks in behind but does not contest, allowing his teammate the victory. Del Toro becomes only the second Mexican to win a Tour de France stage, following Raul Alcala in 1986 — and he does it in just his second Tour appearance. GC moves to fourth at +0:16 after absorbing the 10-second stage bonus.
