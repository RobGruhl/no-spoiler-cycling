§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Giulio Pellizzari's 2026 read as the year Red Bull-Bora-Hansgrohe's quiet investment in the 22-year-old Italian climber began to look obvious. Tirreno-Adriatico was the week he proved it across seven stages: third on Stage 2's wet gravel (same time as Van der Poel and Del Toro), second on Stage 4 in the reduced front group, then fourth on the queen stage at Camerino — a top-five GC alongside three Italian podium-tier stage finishes. At the Giro — his home Grand Tour — he confirmed the promise: a tireless presence in the mountains, never more so than on the Dolomite queen stage (Stage 19), where he made the break over the Passo Giau and then sacrificed his own result to pace teammate Jai Hindley back after a setback, helping Hindley onto the final podium. In June he reinforced his climbing credentials at the Tour of Slovenia, helping force the decisive selection on the Vršič queen stage and finishing second to teammate Florian Lipowitz hand in hand for a Red Bull one-two — sitting second overall by four seconds with a stage to ride. A selfless, race-shaping season from one of Italy's brightest young climbers.
