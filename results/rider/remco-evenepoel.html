§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Remco Evenepoel's first season in Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe colours opened with a controlled statement of intent at the Volta a la Comunitat Valenciana in early February. He won the Stage 2 individual time trial from Carlet to Alginet to build his GC platform, then proved the strongest climber on the Stage 4 queen stage to Teulada Moraira, taking the leader's jersey from Biniam Girmay and the overall victory by 31 seconds over João Almeida. It was the textbook punchy-stage-race formula — win the time trial, win the decisive mountain day — executed cleanly to open the year and signal he had settled quickly into his new team.

By the time the Tour de France reached Stage 15, Evenepoel had established himself as third in the GC (+5:04 to Pogacar). The stage to the Plateau de Solaison rewrote his Tour story: when Jonas Vingegaard crashed and abandoned with 20 kilometres to go, the race distilled to three riders — Pogacar, Isaac del Toro, and Evenepoel. Pogacar set the final-climb pace, del Toro attacked twice inside the last kilometre, and Evenepoel covered both moves before launching his own sprint half a kilometre from the line. Pogacar did not contest. It was Evenepoel's first major mountain stage victory at the Tour de France — and it moved him from third to second overall at +5:00.

Stage 16 — the Tour's only individual time trial — was Evenepoel's stage. The world and Olympic time trial champion rode Évian-les-Bains to Thonon-les-Bains in 32:19, nearly half a minute faster than Pogačar at the line. Back-to-back stage victories — mountain stage and time trial — underline the breadth of his 2026 Tour, and cut his GC gap to 4:32 with three Alpine days to come.
