§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Jay Vine's 2026 began with the Tour Down Under overall — a textbook UAE GC win on the team's reliable summer-Aussie template, with the closing kicker the now-iconic kangaroo collision on Stage 5 that gave him a broken wrist on the way to the jersey. The wrist was the story for two months: he sat out the early-spring block, returned to racing at Volta a Catalunya in March still managing pain, and then crashed again on Stage 3 of that race and abandoned — eight weeks of recovery cut short by a second incident inside the wrist's healing window.

The Giro d'Italia in May was meant to be the rebuild's payoff: a return to GC on a stage race Vine had targeted for years. Instead, Stage 2 in Bulgaria produced UAE's worst day of 2026 — a mass crash inside 21 km to go that took out four of the team's riders. Vine fractured an elbow and suffered a concussion, was immediately withdrawn, and joined Soler (fractured pelvis), Adam Yates (concussion), and Baroncini on UAE's mid-May injury list. The TDU win is the year's bright spot; everything since has been recovery interrupted by re-injury.
