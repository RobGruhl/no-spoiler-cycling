§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Jan Christen confirmed his status as one of cycling's brightest young all-rounders in early 2026, winning the overall and a stage at the AlUla Tour, finishing 2nd at the Faun-Ardèche Classic and 4th overall at the Vuelta a Andalucía. The 21-year-old Swiss arrived at the Giro d'Italia in service of UAE Team Emirates-XRG, a role that grew after the team's plans were upended by the Bulgarian crash that decimated their lineup. Stepping up, he rode aggressively into the breakaways, notably featuring in the move on Stage 17 in the high mountains. His Giro is a developing-talent arc: gaining three-week experience, supporting the team's revised ambitions, and showing himself at the front when the road tilted up. With the race heading to Rome on 31 May behind leader Vingegaard, Christen banks valuable Grand Tour mileage. His spring punch and stage-race results mark him as a future leader for UAE.

Jan 27, 2026
AlUla Tour
LEADER P1 Overall winner · UAE Team Emirates - XRG

Christen's AlUla Tour was a comeback in five acts. He crashed twice on the windswept Stage 1, missed the front echelon and lost 11 seconds, a deficit that hung over his whole week. He showed his hand early on the Stage 3 summit, attacking at 2.2 km, but Eulálio bridged and Voisard out-kicked the group at the line. He stayed patient through the Stage 4 sprint day, then produced the decisive ride on Stage 5: after Higuita attacked first and race leader Voisard cracked, Christen bridged to the leaders on the summit straight and went solo with 4 km to go, winning the stage by 11 seconds and seizing the overall. At 21 it was the first stage-race victory of his career — and his seventh pro win — plus the white young-rider jersey.

  • Stage 1: crashed twice (once ~15 km to go), chased back but lost 11s in the crosswinds

  • 4 kmStage 5: bridged to the leaders then attacked solo on the summit straight to win the stage and the overall
