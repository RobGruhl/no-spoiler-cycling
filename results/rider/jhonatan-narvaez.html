§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Jhonatan Narváez's 2026 became the season he turned into UAE Team Emirates-XRG's irreplaceable stage-hunter, and the Giro d'Italia was the stage on which he proved it. After the team's Bulgarian crash disaster gutted its roster — Vine, Soler, Adam Yates and Baroncini all out — Narváez carried the campaign almost single-handedly, sprinting from a reduced group to win Stage 4 in Cosenza, then doubling up on Stage 8 in Fermo and Stage 11 in Chiavari for a remarkable three stage wins by the race's final week. The Ecuadorian's versatility was the through-line: he won from breakaways and on punchy uphill finishes alike, the kind of all-terrain opportunism that also kept him in the fight for the maglia ciclamino against pure sprinter Paul Magnier. The trio of victories pushed his Giro career tally to five individual stages across 2020, 2024 and 2026, cementing him as one of the most prolific stage-hunters in the modern peloton. With the Giro running to its Rome finish on 31 May, Narváez had already authored UAE's defining storyline of the race and set himself up as a marked man for the breakaways and one-day classics to come.

May 20, 2026
Giro d'Italia — Stage 11: Porcari → Chiavari
GRAND TOUR
STAGE HUNTER P1 · UAE Team Emirates XRG

Narváez bridged into the move on the Passo del Termine and was a constant presence at the front through the closing climbs. He marked Diego Ulissi's late attack inside the final 2 km and then sat on Enric Mas's wheel as the Spaniard accelerated to Cogorno, letting Mas do almost all the work over the top. In the two-up finale at Chiavari he came around comfortably to take the sprint, claiming his third stage win of this Giro.
