§ 01 · The season so far

Arc

Lotte Kopecky underlined her status as the most complete one-day racer in the women's peloton across the 2026 spring. After a win at Nokere Koerse earlier in the week, she delivered the headline result of her early season at Milano-Sanremo Donne, reading the chaotic race perfectly: distanced briefly on the Cipressa, she fought back, made the decisive Poggio selection off Puck Pieterse's move, then won the five-up sprint on the Via Roma for another Monument. The cobbled classics didn't reward her in kind — fourth at the Ronde van Vlaanderen (+1:04) and fourth at Paris-Roubaix Femmes (+1:30) left her just off the podium in both — but a Monument win and back-to-back top-fours in the biggest cobbled races confirmed a spring of relentless front-of-race racing.

MONUMENT

A former Roubaix winner (2024) caught on the wrong side of the day's only decisive split. Mar 21, 2026
Milano-Sanremo Donne
MONUMENT
LEADER P1 3h 47m 17s · Team SD Worx - Protime

A masterclass in reading Milano-Sanremo. Kopecky was briefly distanced when Niewiadoma drove the Cipressa but fought back to the bunch. On the Poggio she was one of only four able to follow Pieterse's decisive acceleration, then sat composed in the front five, letting Włodarczyk and Pieterse do the work into Sanremo. When Pieterse opened the sprint on the Via Roma, Kopecky came off her wheel and powered past for her biggest win of the young season and another Monument to her palmarès, fresh off a win at Nokere Koerse earlier in the week.
