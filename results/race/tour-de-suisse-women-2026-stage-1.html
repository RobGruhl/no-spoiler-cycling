§ 01 · The stage

De Vries lands her first pro win on a demanding opener

The 2026 Tour de Suisse Women began with a hard 109 km circuit-based road stage in and around Sondrio, with repeated ascents of the Triangia climb stripping the bunch back. On the second-to-last pass of Triangia, Femke de Vries and Lauren Dickson broke clear of the favourites' group and committed to the move together. Despite several attacks behind from the GC contenders, the pair held their advantage to the line.

De Vries proved the stronger in the two-up sprint, beating Dickson for her first professional victory and the first leader's jersey of the race. Cédrine Kerbaol led the chase home 29 seconds back for third, ahead of a compact group containing Kim Le Court-Pienaar, Sarah Van Dam, Elisa Longo Borghini, Steffi Häberlin, Marlen Reusser and Kasia Niewiadoma, all at 38 seconds. The win also handed De Vries the early leads in the mountains and points classifications.

§ 02 · GC impact

De Vries takes the first yellow jersey

Femke de Vries leads the general classification after stage 1, with Lauren Dickson second at 4 seconds (a deficit set by time bonuses) and Cédrine Kerbaol third at 35 seconds. The main GC favourites — Le Court-Pienaar (+0:48), Longo Borghini (+0:48), Reusser (+0:48) and Niewiadoma — all finished together in the chasing group and sit clustered just under a minute down.
