§ 01 · The stage

Pithie wins a crash-shattered sprint

Stage 3 from Habay to Vaux-sur-Sûre (176.9 km) was a mostly flat day pointed at the sprinters and puncheurs, with a slight rise to the line making for a tricky finish. The day's breakaway — Jonah Killy, George Wood, Álvaro Sagrado, Filippo Conca, Jarno Bellens and Senne Thonnon — never threatened and was reeled in just outside the final 10 km. With every team saving its cards for a bunch sprint, a crash just outside the final kilometre split the peloton completely, leaving only a handful of riders at the front. Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe had numbers there: Jordi Meeus and Danny van Poppel drove the lead-out and Laurence Pithie sprinted to victory, beating INEOS's Kim Heiduk to the line with Krists Neilands (NSN) third. All three picked up valuable bonus seconds for the overall.

§ 02 · GC impact

Heiduk moves into the lead as the GC tightens

The crash and the bonus seconds reshuffled a GC already separated by only a few seconds. Kim Heiduk took over the race lead heading into stage 4, holding a three-second margin over Jordi Meeus, Ben Oliver and Laurence Pithie — leaving the overall poised for the bonus-second skirmishes of the final two days.

Storylines from the stage

🇧🇪 Jordi Meeus Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
HELPER —

Rather than chasing the win himself, Meeus turned lead-out man, working with Danny van Poppel to deliver Laurence Pithie through the chaos of the crash-split finale. It was a selfless ride from the stage-1 winner that kept Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe at the front of both the stage and the GC, with Meeus himself still inside three seconds of the new race leader.
