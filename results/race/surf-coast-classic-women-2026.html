Surf Coast Classic Women
2026

The 2026 women's Surf Coast Classic (Lorne to Torquay, scheduled 28 January) was cancelled owing to extreme heat and high bushfire risk in the Great Ocean Road region. Organisers replaced it with the 40km 'Cadel's Criterium' on a 2km circuit in Geelong's Eastern Gardens, won by Martina Fidanza (Visma | Lease a Bike) ahead of the SD Worx-Protime pair of Marta Lach and Barbara Guarischi. The criterium result does not count toward official UCI win tallies.

§ 01 · The race in 90 seconds

Bushfire risk cancels the road race; Fidanza wins the stand-in crit

OPENINGThe women's Surf Coast Classic was scheduled as a coastal road race from Lorne to Torquay on Wednesday 28 January, a crosswind-prone day that often splits the bunch. It never started.

UNFOLDSOrganisers and the Surf Coast Shire cancelled the race owing to extreme heat and a heightened bushfire and weather warning across the Great Ocean Road region, with the men's race called off the next day for the same reasons.

DECIDEDTo preserve a race day, organisers staged 'Cadel's Criterium', a 40km event on a technical 2km circuit at Eastern Gardens in Geelong.

FINALEThe criterium came down to a bunch sprint, with Martina Fidanza (Visma | Lease a Bike) holding off the SD Worx-Protime duo of Marta Lach and Barbara Guarischi. As a replacement event, the win is unofficial and does not appear on the UCI calendar.

§ 03 · Team-by-team

Who pressed, who missed

Visma | Lease a Bike
Fidanza wins the stand-in crit

With the road race scrapped, Martina Fidanza delivered Visma | Lease a Bike's first win of 2026 in the substitute Cadel's Criterium, finding her lead-out in the final 500m of a chaotic sprint to beat two SD Worx-Protime riders. The result carries no UCI points but opened the team's account for the year.

SD Worx-Protime
Second and third in the crit

SD Worx-Protime placed both Marta Lach and Barbara Guarischi on the podium of the replacement criterium, Lach taking second after being squeezed off the lead-out and finding an alternate route to the front.

§ 06 · Aftermath

An Australian-summer race lost to fire risk

The women's Surf Coast Classic had risen in calendar status for 2026, but extreme heat and bushfire danger made the coastal route untenable. Cadel's Criterium kept the women's peloton racing ahead of the Cadel Evans Great Ocean Road Race weekend, but the official Surf Coast Classic has no 2026 winner.
