§ 01 · The stage

Pedersen and Lidl-Trek commandeer a scorching Pyrenean opener as Træen steals yellow in the break.

Stage 4 rolls 181.9 km from the medieval fortress of Carcassonne to Foix in fierce heat — temperatures touching 40°C — with the UCI permitting riders to carry bags of ice and water in place of individual bottles. A 14-rider move goes clear inside the first 15 km featuring Mads Pedersen, Mathias Vacek, Pablo Castrillo, Kévin Vauquelin, Jasper Stuyven, Pascal Eenkhoorn, and others. Within another 15 km a second wave of 20 riders bridges across, swelling the break to 34 and delivering Biniam Girmay, Jasper Philipsen, Romain Grégoire, Michael Matthews, Quinn Simmons, Sean Quinn, Torstein Træen, and Raúl García Pierna into the escapees. The advantage swells past 10 minutes over a passive peloton happy to let non-GC riders share the glory in the heat.

At the intermediate sprint in Quillan, Girmay edges out Philipsen and Pedersen to claim the bonus seconds, briefly threatening Pogacar's grip on the green jersey. On the Col de Coudons, Jan Tratnik attacks sharply; Mathias Vacek follows immediately and García Pierna bridges across, the trio briefly opening a 1-minute gap before being swallowed back on the plateau after the summit. The break then crests the final obstacle, the Col de Montségur, and attacks reduce the leading group to ten: Pedersen, Quinn Simmons, Vacek, Sean Quinn, Vauquelin, Ramses Debruyne, Castrillo, García Pierna, Marco Frigo, and Træen. Behind them, Girmay and Philipsen — both sapped by the heat — fade badly and finish over 22 minutes adrift of the stage winner.

With 30 km remaining on the descent and approach to Foix, Lidl-Trek takes full command. Simmons and Vacek drive tempo while Pedersen is kept sheltered. Isolated attacks from García Pierna and Castrillo are neutralised before Pedersen opens his sprint in the Foix finishing straight and powers clear by several bike lengths, the clock reading 4h 10' 45''. Meanwhile the peloton — containing all of Pogacar, Vingegaard, Evenepoel and the complete GC group — trundles in 12 minutes 59 seconds later, having collectively accepted the time loss in the extreme heat.

Træen, who started the day 5 minutes 6 seconds behind Pogacar, crosses the line 8th in the break's sprint to bank the same 12:59 gap over the yellow-jersey group. The arithmetic tips him into the overall lead — Træen now heads Pogacar by 7 minutes 53 seconds. It is the first yellow jersey in Uno-X Mobility team history. The 30-year-old Norwegian, who survived testicular cancer discovered through an anomalous doping control in 2022, wept on the podium: 'I really don't understand what's happening right now.'

§ 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇩🇪 Mads Pedersen Lidl-Trek
STAGE HUNTER P1

Pedersen slots into the early break alongside team-mates Simmons and Vacek and spends the entire stage in the ideal position — sheltered by his colleagues on every climb while the heat whittles the group down. On the Col de Montségur, he deftly avoids the dangerous sub-moves and emerges in the final 10-man group. The sprint in Foix is a formality: he times his run, opens a gap that nobody can close and wins by several lengths. His third career Tour stage victory, and his first since 2023. The stage win also earns him enough sprint points to take the green jersey (points classification) heading into Stage 5.

🇫🇷 Kévin Vauquelin Netcompany-INEOS Cycling Team
STAGE HUNTER P6 s.t.

Vauquelin is among the original 14-rider move and survives all selections to contest the final sprint. A 6th-place finish in the 10-man breakaway is a solid day's work in difficult heat, taking him comfortably ahead of the peloton.

🇪🇷 Biniam Girmay NSN Cycling Team
STAGE HUNTER P167 +22:08

Girmay enters the day's break and marks his presence at the Quillan intermediate sprint, beating Philipsen and Pedersen to claim bonus seconds. But the relentless 40°C heat proves too much on the Col de Montségur, and he is dropped from the leading group. He eventually trails in 167th, more than 22 minutes behind the winner — a rare bad day on a stage that exposed the physical cost of racing in extreme conditions.

🇧🇪 Jasper Philipsen Alpecin-Premier Tech
STAGE HUNTER P175 +22:41

Philipsen reaches the Quillan intermediate sprint in contention, finishing 2nd behind Girmay, but the heat and the climbs combine to drop him well before the final selection. He eventually finishes 175th, over 22 minutes down — a difficult day for the points jersey challenger.

🇫🇷 Romain Grégoire Groupama-FDJ United
STAGE HUNTER P22 +2:27

Grégoire makes it into the enlarged break but is distanced during the Col de Montségur selection, settling into the secondary group that finishes 2:27 down. A decent ride but he misses the 10-rider finale.

🇦🇺 Michael Matthews Team Jayco AlUla
STAGE HUNTER P11 +2:27

Matthews gets into the large break but cannot stay with the leading 10 over Col de Montségur, coming home 11th in the second group at +2:27. Still a useful day ahead of the main peloton.

🇸🇮 Tadej Pogacar UAE Team Emirates XRG
LEADER P33 +12:59

Pogacar loses the yellow jersey he claimed just 24 hours earlier, marking the third consecutive Tour where he has failed to hold the maillot jaune past Stage 4. UAE Emirates allow the break to go, choosing not to chase in blistering heat, and the entire peloton crosses the line 12:59 adrift. On aggregate GC Pogacar sits 4th at 7:53 behind the new leader Træen. The race is still wide open — the first serious mountain stage is Stage 6 to Gavarnie.

🇩🇪 Jonas Vingegaard Team Visma | Lease a Bike
LEADER P43 +12:59

Vingegaard finishes with the entire GC peloton at +12:59, dropping to 5th on GC, equal with Pogacar at 7:53 behind Træen. Like his rival, Visma accept the time loss in the heat and save energy for the high mountains. Neither team contests the breakaway.

🇧🇪 Remco Evenepoel Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER P57 +12:59

Evenepoel rolls in with the GC group at +12:59, holding 7th overall at +8:16. A controlled day before the real mountains.

🇲🇽 Isaac del Toro UAE Team Emirates XRG
HELPER P34 +12:59

Del Toro finishes alongside Pogacar in the peloton. His role as Pogacar's lead-out anchor from Stage 3 continues to define the UAE strategy; no climbing duties today.

🏳️ Juan Ayuso Lidl-Trek
LEADER P38 +12:59

Ayuso finishes with the peloton at +12:59, 9th overall at +8:20. A passive stage for the Spaniard, though he has team-mates Pedersen, Simmons, and Vacek all moving up the GC through the break.

🏳️ Florian Lipowitz Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER P60 +12:59

Lipowitz finishes in the GC peloton, 11th overall at +8:46. A static stage for the young German climber ahead of the Pyrenean showdown.

🇫🇷 Paul Seixas Decathlon CMA CGM Team
LEADER P50 +12:59

Seixas arrives with the peloton at +12:59, retaining 10th overall at +8:41. The 19-year-old remains in the top 10 despite the day's upheaval in the break.
