§ 01 · The race in 90 seconds

Third Strade in a row, third winner who attacked over Monte Sante Marie

OPENING201 km, shortened from the usual 215 to fit 64 km of gravel rather than the recent ~85 km. Whether or not the shortening was supposed to make the race tactically richer, it didn't change the script: a modest early break, the favourites' teams rotating control, the race kept on tempo through the opening four sterrato sectors.

UNFOLDSMonte Sante Marie — Sector 10, the canonical Strade Bianche selection — did exactly what it always does. UAE rode the front, Pogačar attacked, the field exploded. The decisive grouping over the top: Pogačar, Seixas, Del Toro, then a chase of Pidcock, Jorgenson, Grégoire, Kron, Van Aert.

DECIDEDOn a later short gravel sector — sources differ on which one, but it was inside the closing 25 km — Pogačar accelerated again. Del Toro couldn't follow. Seixas could, but only for a few kilometres. By the time the leading pair entered the closing 15 km, Pogačar had ridden alone and Seixas was holding what daylight he could.

FINALEPogačar took the Via Santa Caterina climb solo, into Piazza del Campo and a third Strade Bianche win on the way to what would become a three-Monument spring. Seixas had a minute back held alone; Del Toro completed the UAE 1-3 from the chase. Pidcock, Jorgenson, Van Aert filled positions 7-10 from the bunch at 1:30.

§ 02 · Decisive moments

Where the race tilted

  1. 01 55 km to go Sector 10 — Monte Sante Marie
    Pogačar attacks on the canonical sector
    Strade Bianche's selecting sector has historically been the only place the race actually splits. It did so again. Over the top: Pogačar with Seixas and Del Toro for company. Everyone else was now chasing.
  2. 02 20 km to go Late gravel sector
    Del Toro cracks, then Seixas
    A second acceleration peeled Del Toro back to the chase first, then dropped Seixas a few kilometres later. From there, Pogačar rode alone to Siena.
  3. 03 0.5 km to go Via Santa Caterina
    Solo to Piazza del Campo
    The 16% wall into Siena is normally the day's final selection; this time it was a victory lap. Pogačar arrived alone, Seixas a minute back also alone.
§ 03 · Team-by-team

Who pressed, who missed

UAE Team Emirates - XRG
1-3 on the gravel

Pogačar attacks, Del Toro holds long enough to ride the chase and salvage third. The UAE pattern for 2026 — leader and a young protected co-card — got its first Monument-level expression. Del Toro's third place is a result Pogačar himself would have called a podium had it been his own three years ago.

Riders: POGAČAR Tadej
Decathlon CMA CGM Team
Seixas's coming-out at 19

Paul Seixas, the Tour de l'Avenir champion making his Strade debut, followed Pogačar over Monte Sante Marie and held on long enough to take a solo second. Decathlon's bet on the 19-year-old as a Monument-tier rider lands two months before Liège would confirm it again.

Riders: SEIXAS Paul
Visma | Lease a Bike
Top 10 with Jorgenson and Van Aert, never in the move

Visma rode two leaders in Jorgenson (the day's actual finisher) and Van Aert (returning to gravel after a long absence). Neither could match Pogačar's Monte Sante Marie selection. Jorgenson 8th, Van Aert 10th — clean top-10 ride, no Monument bid.

Riders: VAN AERT Wout, JORGENSON Matteo
Pinarello-Q36.5 Pro Cycling
Pidcock's debut in new colours, top 10

Tom Pidcock's first major race in Pinarello-Q36.5 kit — formerly INEOS, now riding for the smaller WorldTour outfit. 7th at Strade is a clean opening statement; two weeks later he'd come within centimetres of beating Pogačar at Sanremo.

§ 06 · Aftermath

First Monument-spring weekend; first appearance of the Seixas problem

Strade is not a Monument but is treated like one. Pogačar's third title here makes him the most successful rider in the race's short history. The bigger long-run story leaving Siena was Paul Seixas — a 19-year-old who'd just turned the most-watched outsider on the spring calendar into a confirmed Monument-class climber, with two more podiums to come by April's end.
