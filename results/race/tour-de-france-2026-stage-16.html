§ 01 · The stage

Evenepoel's TT masterclass brings back-to-back stage wins.

Stage 16 — the only individual time trial of the 2026 Tour — sent riders 26.1 kilometres from Évian-les-Bains along Lake Geneva to Thonon-les-Bains, with a category 2 climb up the Côte de Larringes (16.4km) before a technical descent and fast closing kilometres into town.

Bruno Armirail set an early benchmark of 34:14, quickly bettered by Mattia Cattaneo in 34:01. Mattias Skjelmose then stormed through to take the provisional lead, but with over a minute in hand, the stage was ultimately decided by the last two riders to roll down the start ramp.

Remco Evenepoel, starting second to last, was already 16 seconds quicker than Pogačar at the third checkpoint. He then accelerated through the flat finale to stop the clock at 32:19 — nearly half a minute faster than anyone else. Pogačar, who had a wobble at the same sharp corner near the finish where teammate Lipowitz crashed, crossed in 32:47 for second place.

The tragedy of the day was Florian Lipowitz, who slid into the kerb on a right-hand bend with 6km remaining and was unable to continue, reportedly with a shoulder injury. The German, who had been fifth in the overall standings, abandoned the race — ending what had been a promising Tour debut in a GC top-five spot.

§ 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇧🇪 Remco Evenepoel Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER P1

World and Olympic time trial champion Evenepoel produced the performance of the day, stopping the clock at 32:19 to be nearly half a minute faster than Pogačar. He was already leading at every checkpoint — 16 seconds up on the Slovenian at the third split — and pulled clear through the fast final kilometres. The win made it back-to-back Tour stages after his mountain victory on Stage 15, and cut his GC deficit from 5:00 to 4:32 with three Alpine days to decide the race.

🇸🇮 Tadej Pogačar UAE Team Emirates - XRG
LEADER P2 +0:28

Pogačar rode a controlled and strong TT — second fastest on the day at 32:47 — but could not match the world champion's pace. He had a wobble at the sharp corner near the finish where Lipowitz had crashed, losing a handful of seconds in recovery, but held it together to cross safely. His GC lead shrank by 28 seconds but at 4:32 over Evenepoel heading into the final Alpine trio, yellow remains firmly in Slovenian hands.

🇩🇪 Mattias Skjelmose Lidl-Trek
STAGE HUNTER P3 +1:04

Skjelmose was the best of the pure climbers in the TT, posting third place in 33:23 and holding the provisional lead until Evenepoel rolled down the ramp. The Danish rider moved up a place in the GC to sixth at +10:14 as teammate Florian Lipowitz abandoned, a solid day for Lidl-Trek in what is usually not their optimal terrain.

🇫🇷 Paul Seixas Decathlon CMA CGM Team
GC-CONTENDER P4 +1:16

The young Frenchman rode a composed effort to fourth in the stage, losing only 48 seconds to Pogačar and holding fourth in the GC at +7:11 — 20 seconds behind del Toro. A consistent performance for the revelation of this Tour, who continues to sit within a minute of the podium.

🇲🇽 Isaac Del Toro UAE Team Emirates - XRG
GC-CONTENDER P5 +1:21

Del Toro retained third overall by finishing fifth in the stage, losing 53 seconds to Pogačar — a reasonable result for a pure climber in a TT. His GC gap widened slightly from +5:58 to +6:51 but the white jersey remains his. UAE's one-two in the GC top three means Pogačar has his strongest teammate well-placed heading into the final three mountain stages.

🇳🇱 Thymen Arensman NetCompany INEOS Cycling Team
STAGE HUNTER P7 +1:46

Arensman posted seventh on the stage — a strong individual effort for a rider building his form through this Tour. Outside GC contention but showed his time trial capabilities on a course that suited the versatile Dutch rider.

🏳️ Florian Lipowitz Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER INCIDENT

Lipowitz's Tour ended in heartbreak. Riding fifth in the GC and on course for a career-defining podium, he slid into the kerb on a sharp right-hand bend approximately 6km from the finish, sustaining what appeared to be a shoulder injury. He was unable to continue. His abandonment robbed Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe of their GC ambitions in what had been a resurgent Tour for the German following his 2026 season win at the Tour of Slovenia.

⚠ crash-abandon
🏳️ Juan Ayuso Lidl-Trek
GC-CONTENDER P15 +2:22

Ayuso had a difficult TT, losing around 1:54 to Pogačar and dropping to fifth in the GC at +9:22. The Spaniard's final position of 15th on the stage reflects that this TT profile — rolling rather than purely flat — did not suit his strengths as a pure climber. He will need the final Alpine stages to recover ground on those above him.

🇫🇷 Lenny Martinez Bahrain Victorious
GC-CONTENDER P20 +2:49

Martinez lost 2:21 to Pogačar in the time trial, finishing 20th on the stage and dropping to seventh in the GC at +12:50. Not unexpected for a climber on a 26km rolling TT, and the Frenchman will look to claw back time on the three remaining Alpine stages.
