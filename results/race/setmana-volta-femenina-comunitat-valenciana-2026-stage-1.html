§ 01 · The stage

Vollering converts a 20 km Barx solo into the opening stage and the leader's jersey

The 113 km loop from Gandia back to Gandia hinged on the Alt de Barx, the 6 km climb that crests just under 20 km from the line. FDJ United-SUEZ lit the climb at its base to thin the bunch, and Vollering was the first to attack, going clear over the top with only Antonia Niedermaier able to follow.

When the move stalled, Vollering attacked again with about 18.7 km remaining, dropped Niedermaier and committed to a long solo. She extended the gap across the plateau and the descent into Gandia and won alone by a clear margin. Behind, a reduced chase of around nine riders sprinted for the minor places: Maeva Squiban took second and Viktória Chladoňová third, both at +52". The win, plus the time bonus, handed Vollering the first leader's jersey of the race.

§ 02 · GC impact

Vollering straight into the lead

Vollering leads the GC after Stage 1 with the time bonus on top of her solo margin, ahead of Squiban (+0:56 on GC) and Chladoňová (+0:58), with Niedermaier and Ottestad heading the next group. Chladoňová's ride also put her into the white youth jersey. With a sprint-friendly Stage 2 to come and the queen stage looming, the GC battle was already shaped around Vollering's climbing.
