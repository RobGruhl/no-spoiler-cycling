§ 01 · The stage

Kopecky wins from an SD Worx one-two and takes red

The 114 km stage from Monforte de Lemos to Antas de Ulla was animated by a two-rider attack that was later joined by two more solo riders, with Allione mopping up the mountain points to take over the QOM jersey. Hanson and Jaskulska were the last escapees to be caught before the bunch sprint.

It came down to a reduced bunch sprint, and Team SD Worx - Protime controlled it perfectly: Lotte Kopecky took the win ahead of teammate Anna van der Breggen, with Letizia Paternoster third. The victory and its time bonus moved Kopecky into the red leader's jersey.

§ 02 · GC impact

Kopecky into red, SD Worx in command

Lotte Kopecky took over the overall lead courtesy of the stage win and bonus seconds, with SD Worx now holding the red jersey and looking dominant in the run to the mountains. With two summit finishes still to come in Asturias, the GC battle was set to be reshaped entirely, but for now the Belgian led and her teammate Van der Breggen sat poised among the favourites.

Storylines from the stage

🏳️ Lotte Kopecky Team SD Worx - Protime
STAGE HUNTER P1

After three near-misses, Kopecky finally won, taking the reduced sprint in Antas de Ulla ahead of teammate Van der Breggen to claim the stage and the red leader's jersey. It was the centrepiece of the points-classification campaign she would carry all the way to the green jersey in Madrid-style fashion on the Angliru.

🏳️ Anna van der Breggen Team SD Worx - Protime
LEADER P2 s.t.

Second behind her own teammate, a tidy result that kept the SD Worx GC leader near the top of the standings and within striking distance heading into the decisive mountain stages. Van der Breggen was clearly the team's protected rider for the overall, with Kopecky free to chase stages and points.
