§ 01 · The race in 90 seconds

Philipsen wins the uphill drag to Nokere

OPENING186.4 km from Deinze through the East Flanders countryside, the 80th edition of this 1.Pro semi-classic and a fixture of the build-up to the cobbled Monuments. As ever, the day's break went up the road early; the sprinters' teams kept the leash short, knowing the race almost always comes down to the famous uphill cobbled finish on the Nokereberg.

UNFOLDSThe local finishing circuits and the short, sharp bergs of the closing kilometres did their usual work of thinning the field without ever fully detonating it. The day's escapees were reeled in, and a large, fast group arrived together for the run to Nokere — the scenario the pure sprinters wanted.

DECIDEDThe race was decided in the final 200 metres on the rising, cobbled Nokereberg, where positioning into the bottom of the drag matters as much as raw speed. The fastest men were all there together with the same time.

FINALEPhilipsen timed the uphill effort best to come over the top first, with Jordi Meeus second and Juan Sebastián Molano third, all in the same bunch-sprint time of 4h 16m 19s. Emilien Jeannière and Milan Menten completed the top five.

§ 03 · Team-by-team

Who pressed, who missed

Alpecin - Premier Tech
Philipsen wins the bunch sprint

Alpecin came to a sprinters' semi-classic with the fastest man in the race and got the result the form-sheet demanded. Controlling the day so the break was caught and delivering Philipsen to the foot of the Nokereberg in position is exactly the kind of mid-March work that sharpens a lead-out for the cobbled Monuments to come. A clean, professional win.

Riders: PHILIPSEN Jasper
Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
Meeus second, beaten for speed

Jordi Meeus got himself into the right place for the uphill drag and was beaten only by Philipsen's top end. Second in a 1.Pro sprint is solid positioning and condition work, but on the day the pure speed belonged to Alpecin.

Riders: MEEUS Jordi
UAE Team Emirates - XRG
Molano third, Morgado in the top 10

UAE put two riders in the top ten — Juan Sebastián Molano onto the podium in third and the young António Morgado tenth — a tidy day's racing for a squad using the Flemish week to build legs. The podium without the win is a fair reflection of a sprint where Philipsen had the edge.

§ 06 · Aftermath

A sprinter's tune-up before the cobbles

Nokere Koerse 2026 played to its usual script: a sprinters' semi-classic decided on the rising cobbles of the Nokereberg, with the fastest finisher in the field taking it. For Philipsen and Alpecin, the win was a useful marker of speed and lead-out cohesion with the cobbled Monuments looming. For Meeus, Molano and Jeannière, the placings were respectable form-finding in a deep mid-March sprint field.
