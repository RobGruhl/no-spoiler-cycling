§ 01 · The stage

Merlier doubles up on the hilly day to Szekszárd

The 152.3 km stage to Szekszárd was billed as hilly and offered the faster men a chance to be dropped, but the punchy parcours stayed together enough for a reduced-bunch sprint. Tim Merlier held his form over the lumps and proved fastest again, beating Fernando Gaviria and Juan Sebastián Molano for his second win of the week. The GC men kept their powder dry, leaving Benoît Cosnefroy comfortably in the overall lead ahead of the decisive Pécs stage.

§ 02 · GC impact

No change at the top — Cosnefroy holds the lead

The reduced sprint did not disturb the overall standings; Benoît Cosnefroy retained the leader's jersey heading into the queen stage to Pécs, where the GC would finally be settled.

Storylines from the stage

🇧🇪 Tim Merlier Soudal Quick-Step
STAGE HUNTER P1 stage win

Merlier backed up his opening-day win with a second victory on the hillier stage to Szekszárd, surviving the lumpy course to win a reduced-bunch sprint ahead of Gaviria and Molano. That he could win on a hilly day as well as the pan-flat openers underlined his form, and it kept him firmly in command of the points classification he would go on to win.
