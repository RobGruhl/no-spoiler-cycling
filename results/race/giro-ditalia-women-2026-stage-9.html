Stage 9: Saluzzo → Saluzzo
Giro d'Italia Women 2026

Elisa Longo Borghini won the final-stage four-up sprint in Saluzzo, but the day belonged to Demi Vollering, who overturned a +0:49 deficit to win the Giro overall. After Niedermaier, Longo Borghini and Fisher-Black went clear on the Montoso, Vollering attacked on the last climb, dropped Anna van der Breggen, then bridged across and took a bonus to seal a 30-second GC win and complete the women's Grand Tour Triple Crown.

§ 01 · The stage

Longo Borghini wins in Saluzzo as Vollering steals the Giro

The 145 km final stage looped out from and back to Saluzzo over two major climbs, and Vollering started it +0:49 down on race leader Anna van der Breggen — close enough to attack, far enough to need a perfect day.

On the Montoso (8.9 km at 9.4%, about 90 km out) FDJ - SUEZ shredded the bunch to roughly seven. Over the top, Antonia Niedermaier attacked with Elisa Longo Borghini and Niamh Fisher-Black, building a gap of around 2:15 that briefly put Niedermaier into virtual pink. Lauren Dickson paced van der Breggen's group to keep the leaders at about 1:40.

The race was decided on the final climb, the Colletto di Brondello. Vollering attacked roughly a kilometre from the summit, dropped van der Breggen, and crested first to also secure the mountains classification. She descended, bridged across to the leading trio, and took 6 bonus seconds at the Colletta di Rossana intermediate sprint to move into the virtual overall lead. The four leaders contested the sprint in Saluzzo, where Longo Borghini — the Italian champion — beat Fisher-Black, Niedermaier and Vollering. Van der Breggen, isolated and out of contention, came in +2:23 and lost the maglia rosa on the final day.

§ 02 · GC impact

Vollering wins the Giro by 30 seconds; van der Breggen drops to 3rd

Final GC: Demi Vollering wins overall, Antonia Niedermaier 2nd at +0:30, Anna van der Breggen 3rd at +1:37, Elisa Longo Borghini 4th at +2:44, Niamh Fisher-Black 5th at +3:26, Femke de Vries 6th at +5:07, Isabella Holmgren 7th at +7:10. Vollering also won the mountains jersey; Elisa Balsamo took the points classification, Holmgren the best young rider, and Lidl - Trek the teams classification. Vollering completed the women's Grand Tour Triple Crown in a single season.
