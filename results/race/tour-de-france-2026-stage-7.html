§ 01 · The stage

Merlier times his sprint to perfection on Bordeaux's grand boulevard

Stage 7 crosses 175km of Landes and Gironde countryside from Hagetmau — following the Pyrenean queen stage and a GC reset of historic proportions — to a finish on Bordeaux's wide Garonne riverside boulevard, the most prestigious sprint venue remaining on the modern Tour calendar. The day's break forms early: Baptiste Veistroffer (Lotto Intermarché) and Jakub Otruba roll clear of the peloton through the pine-scented Landes forest, building a lead that the sprint teams keep under control as the Bordeaux wine country comes into view.

Uno-X inject drama at 22km to go when Anders Skaarseth and Jonas Abrahamsen jump clear in an attack designed to stress the leadouts before the sprint. Dylan van Baarle (Soudal Quick-Step) reacts immediately to close the gap, and by 20km to go Veistroffer and Otruba have given up the breakaway by unspoken agreement, rolling in as the peloton swallows them whole. By 10km the bunch is flying at 62km/h, UNO-X pushing to the front while EF attempt to punch clear of Soudal and NSN.

The technical run-in proves demanding: a dual-carriageway section with road furniture at 7.2km, then a series of roundabouts at 6km, the peloton snaking through narrow gaps. At 5km the sprint zone opens; Netcompany INEOS slide Josh Tarling to the front for their man. With 2km remaining, Mads Pedersen and Tim Merlier are both positioned near the front — Merlier still boxed in with 600m to go but tracking Jasper Philipsen with studied patience. In the final 300m Merlier times his move perfectly, threading through the messy, road-wide bunch to win by a clear margin. Wærenskjold outsprints the rest for second; Girmay takes third. A relief for Soudal Quick-Step after Pau, and a statement win for the Belgian on one of cycling's grandest finishing straights.

§ 02 · GC impact

GC unchanged — Pogačar retains yellow, sprint bonuses go to non-GC riders.

Stage 7 is a pure sprinters' day with no bearing on GC. All general classification contenders finished safely in the bunch. Time bonuses were awarded only to sprint specialists: Merlier (−10''), Wærenskjold (−6''), Girmay (−4''). GC after Stage 7: Tadej Pogačar (UAE Team Emirates XRG) leads. Jonas Vingegaard (Team Visma | Lease a Bike) 2nd at +2:42. Isaac del Toro (UAE Team Emirates XRG) 3rd at +3:27. Remco Evenepoel (Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe) 4th at +3:30. § 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇧🇪 Tim Merlier Soudal Quick-Step
STAGE HUNTER P1

Merlier's Soudal Quick-Step squad controls the race through the final 20km, with van Baarle neutralising the Uno-X counter at 22km to go. Merlier finds himself boxed in approaching 600m but stays calm — tracking Philipsen's wheel and waiting for the gap to open. He unleashes his sprint in the final 300m on the Garonne boulevard, winning comfortably despite the messy bunch. He credits his team's positioning and the Wolfpack's patient work through the day. A major confidence boost for Soudal Quick-Step, who were shut out in the Pau sprint two days prior.

🇳🇴 Søren Wærenskjold Uno-X Mobility
STAGE HUNTER P2 s.t.

Wærenskjold delivers an excellent second place for Uno-X in Bordeaux, outsprinting the rest of the field after Merlier takes the win. The Norwegian, who had earlier seen his teammates Skaarseth and Abrahamsen attempt a 22km-to-go counter-attack, demonstrates strong finishing speed to hold off Girmay for the runner-up spot.

🇪🇷 Biniam Girmay NSN Cycling Team
STAGE HUNTER P3 s.t.

Girmay takes third place for NSN Cycling Team in the Bordeaux sprint, his team's best result of the week-one sprinting opportunities. He had been flagged before the stage as a key contender on a finish suited to his punchy sprint style, and delivers on that billing with a podium finish.

🇧🇪 Jasper Philipsen Alpecin-Premier Tech
STAGE HUNTER P5 s.t.

Philipsen finishes 5th in Bordeaux, a below-par result for the Belgian, who Merlier was watching closely throughout the final kilometre. Merlier credited keeping an eye on Philipsen even while boxed in at 600m, suggesting Alpecin's sprinter was the pre-race favourite. A day where his lead-out didn't deliver the position he needed.

🇩🇪 Mads Pedersen Lidl-Trek
STAGE HUNTER P9 s.t.

Pedersen finishes 9th in Bordeaux, a solid if unspectacular result for the green jersey holder. He pushes to the front in the final 2km but cannot penetrate the melee of faster pure sprinters in the concluding 300m. He retains the points classification jersey heading into Stage 8, his season-long strength in intermediate sprints keeping him comfortably ahead in that competition.
