Stage 9: Malemort → Ussel
Tour de France 2026

Mathieu van der Poel wins a four-up sprint in Ussel after a 154.6 km stage (shortened from 185.5 km due to a red heat-wave alert), beating Tobias Halland Johannessen and Tom Pidcock who overcame a mid-stage mechanical. GC unchanged — Pogačar retains yellow with the same gaps heading into the rest day before Stage 10 at Le Lioran.

§ 01 · The stage

Van der Poel wins heat-shortened Ussel battle in a four-up sprint

Stage 9 was reduced from 185.5 km to 154.6 km by race directors due to a red canicule (heat-wave) alert in the Corrèze. From the shortened neutralised start in Malemort, early attackers — Quinten Hermans, Stefano Oldani, Alex Kirsch, Georg Steinhauser, and Ewen Costiou — were brought back before the intermediate sprint, which Mads Pedersen took ahead of Biniam Girmay. Mathieu van der Poel attempted to attack immediately after but could not get away.

With just under 100 km remaining, a large breakaway finally formed: Van der Poel, Tom Pidcock, Lennert Van Eetvelt, Marc Hirschi, Tobias Halland Johannessen, Tobias Foss, Derek Gee, Quinn Simmons, Alex Baudin, Valentin Paret-Peintre, Pablo Castrillo, Lars Craps, Ion Izagirre, Ewen Costiou, Jordan Jegat, and Clément Braz Afonso. Inside the break, Johannessen and Simmons attacked on the Suc au May climb, building a 30-second advantage. Pidcock gave chase with Gee and Van Eetvelt, while Van der Poel, Castrillo, and Baudin bridged across. The six chasers caught Johannessen and Simmons in the final kilometre of the climb, and the group of eight crested the summit 1'20'' ahead of the peloton.

UAE Team Emirates led the bunch chase, later joined by Netcompany INEOS. The gap dipped below 1 minute on the Côte de la Croix de Pey before steadying near the 1-minute mark for several kilometres. On Mont Bessou, with 30 seconds separating the escapees from the peloton, Van der Poel accelerated sharply. He reached the summit with only Johannessen and Pidcock — the Briton having had a mechanical and then regained contact. Baudin also rejoined. Simmons and Gee sat up, leaving four at the front with 45 seconds over the peloton and 10 km to go.

The gap held as the four entered Ussel. Van der Poel led onto the rising finishing straight and launched his sprint inside the final 200 metres. Johannessen responded but could never come alongside. Pidcock held on for third, Baudin fourth. Behind, Filippo Ganna won the peloton sprint 6 seconds down in 5th, Mads Pedersen 6th, Michael Matthews 7th. Pogačar crossed safely in 11th to retain the yellow jersey ahead of a rest day.

§ 02 · GC impact

GC unchanged — Pogačar retains yellow with the same gaps going into the rest day.

Stage 9 was a breakaway day; all general classification contenders finished safely in the peloton with no time lost between them. Time bonuses of −10'', −6'', −4'' went to Van der Poel, Johannessen, and Pidcock respectively — none are GC contenders. GC after Stage 9: Tadej Pogačar (UAE Team Emirates XRG) leads. § 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇳🇱 Mathieu van der Poel Alpecin-Premier Tech
STAGE HUNTER P1

A consummate performance from Van der Poel on a stage that perfectly suited his all-round engine: long-range efforts, repeated accelerations, and a finish-line sprint. After driving the early momentum in the peloton and failing to establish an early solo move, he waited for the right moment before joining the decisive break. On Mont Bessou he detonated the group of eight with a sharp acceleration, arriving at the summit with only Johannessen and Pidcock, then controlled the final 10 km before uncorking a powerful sprint in the last 200 metres — too fast for even Johannessen to match. His first Tour stage win of 2026 comes at the ideal moment, bridging the flat opening week to the Massif Central mountains.

🏳️ Tom Pidcock Pinarello-Q36.5 Pro Cycling Team
STAGE HUNTER P3 s.t.

Third place is an excellent result for Pidcock, made more impressive by a mid-race mechanical that threatened to eliminate him from the leading group on Mont Bessou. He chased back after the brief setback and rejoined the four-man front group in time for the final sprint. In the Ussel uphill finish he could not match Van der Poel's explosive speed but comfortably held third. For Pinarello-Q36.5, it is the team's best Tour result of 2026 — a podium that shows Pidcock is back near his best.

🇫🇷 Alex Baudin EF Education - EasyPost
STAGE HUNTER P4 s.t.

A strong showing from Baudin on a stage suited to his punchy climbing abilities. He was part of the main breakaway from the start and played a key bridging role on Suc au May, linking up with Van der Poel and Castrillo to catch the Johannessen/Simmons duo. Baudin then survived the Mont Bessou selection — briefly dropped when Van der Poel's surge split the group, but regained contact to form the decisive four-man lead group. He was unable to challenge Van der Poel in the uphill sprint but secured a well-earned 4th place, EF's best stage result of the 2026 Tour.

🇮🇹 Filippo Ganna Netcompany INEOS Cycling Team
DOMESTIQUE P5 +0:06

Ganna's stage day was primarily spent driving the Netcompany INEOS chase in the final 40 km alongside UAE Team Emirates, helping to keep the breakaway's lead manageable. He was rewarded with the fastest time in the peloton sprint — 5th on the stage, 6 seconds behind the winner. An efficient double role: chase engine and peloton sprint winner.

🇩🇪 Mads Pedersen Lidl-Trek
STAGE HUNTER P6 +0:06

Pedersen won the intermediate sprint ahead of Girmay early in the day, then remained in the peloton as his teammate Quinn Simmons participated in the breakaway and later sat up to aid the chase. Pedersen finished 6th in the peloton sprint, content to collect points on a day where the win was always likely to go to the break.

🇦🇺 Michael Matthews Team Jayco AlUla
STAGE HUNTER P7 +0:06

Matthews finished 7th in the peloton sprint, confirming he remains one of the strongest finishers in the bunch on stages where the breakaway contests the win. On a day reduced to 154.6 km by the heat protocol, he conserved well and timed his effort in the sprint for 7th — a solid points-race result on a day with limited GC relevance.
