§ R · GRAND TOUR · France

Tour de France
2026

The 113th Tour de France opened in Barcelona with a history-making team time trial where Visma | Lease a Bike took the stage and Jonas Vingegaard claimed the first yellow jersey. Stage 2 saw UAE produce back-to-back stage wins, with Isaac del Toro prevailing on the Montjuïc circuit. Stage 3 (Granollers → Les Angles) delivered the first Pyrenean summit finish: Tadej Pogacar won on the Les Angles ramp and takes the yellow jersey — he and Vingegaard are identically level on time entering Stage 4.

§ 01 · The race in 90 seconds

Pogacar in yellow after the first Pyrenean summit as the GC battle takes its first decisive shape.

OPENINGThe 2026 Tour de France makes history with its first-ever opening team time trial since 1971 and first Spanish Grand Départ in decades. Barcelona's seafront, city centre, and the iconic Montjuïc hill form the 19.6 km course, where teams race as units but each rider earns their own GC time — a format borrowed from Paris-Nice. The Catalan crowds pack Montjuïc's slopes as 23 teams attempt to establish early GC advantages. Caja Rural-Seguros RGA set the early benchmark at 22'59'' as local heroes, quickly overhauled by French teams before the GC favourites arrive. Netcompany Ineos surge to provisional lead at 21'55'' — but a puncture for Kévin Vauquelin forces Filippo Ganna to carry the team alone over the Montjuïc finale. Lidl-Trek impress with Ayuso at 22'03'', Red Bull-Bora confirm strength with Evenepoel at +19'' on Ineos. Then Visma | Lease a Bike deliver the day's masterclass: Vingegaard rides clear of his own teammates on the final climb to record 21'47''. UAE complete the podium, with Pogačar fastest over the last 3.7 km but 12 seconds in arrears.

UNFOLDSStage 2 (168.5 km, Tarragona → Barcelona) brings the first road race of the Tour back to the Montjuïc circuit. A three-man breakaway — Frank van den Broek, Alex Molenaar, Felix Engelhardt — builds over three minutes before being caught entering the closing Montjuïc laps. With approximately 60 km remaining, Isaac del Toro suffers a mechanical and waits roadside for a replacement bike. He rejoins within 10 km, just in time for the decisive finale. UAE systematically control the Montjuïc circuit laps: Brandon McNulty sets a fierce tempo on the first ascent, Pogačar and three teammates command the second, and Visma's Jorgenson counters on the penultimate lap. Mathieu van der Poel is among those dropped as the lead group reduces to around 15 on the final ascent. Mattias Skjelmose attacks but del Toro responds immediately, then accelerates through a corner to open a decisive gap. Pogačar tucks in behind his teammate, controlling rather than contesting, and del Toro crosses the line first — his first Tour stage win in his second Tour appearance, and Mexico's first Tour stage since Raul Alcala in 1986. Stage 3 (195.9 km, Granollers → Les Angles) delivers the race's first genuine summit finish into the Pyrenees. A 19-rider breakaway eventually goes clear, with EF Education-EasyPost's Alex Baudin the star of the escape — cresting the Col de Toses first to take the polka-dot jersey and attacking again on the Col du Calvaire. Baudin is caught 11 km from Les Angles. On the final ramp del Toro delivers a long lead-out for Pogacar, who sprints to victory with 200 metres to go and crosses the line 2 seconds ahead of Vingegaard. The 10-second bonus (versus 6 for Vingegaard) cancels Visma's 6-second GC advantage exactly — the two leaders are level on time entering Stage 4, with Pogacar wearing yellow on stage-win countback. Skjelmose loses 1:31 on the stage, dropping from fifth to fifteenth GC.

§ 02 · Decisive moments

Where the race tilted

  1. 01 3.7 km to go Montjuïc Olympic climb — Stage 1
    Vingegaard solos away to claim the first yellow jersey
    Using the individual-timing TTT format, Vingegaard attacks on the Montjuïc ascent, separating from his own teammates and finishing in 21'47'' — eight seconds faster than Filippo Ganna and 12 seconds ahead of Pogačar. The move immediately establishes Visma's dominance from day one.
  2. 02 Stage 1 — Kévin Vauquelin puncture
    Vauquelin flat disrupts Ineos's TTT plan
    A puncture near checkpoint 3 forces Ganna to carry Ineos alone over the finale; despite the mechanical, Ganna's 21'55'' holds the team in second place.
  3. 03 60 km to go Open road — Stage 2
    Del Toro mechanical and roadside wait
    A radio miscommunication leaves del Toro waiting for a replacement bike with 60 km remaining. He rejoins the peloton in under 10 km and goes on to win the stage.
  4. 04 1.5 km to go Côte du Château de Montjuïc — Stage 2
    Del Toro counters Skjelmose and attacks through a corner
    Skjelmose's acceleration is chased and snuffed out by del Toro, who then carries remarkable momentum through a corner to open a gap nobody can close. Pogačar controls behind, and the stage goes to UAE for a second consecutive day.
  5. 05 0.2 km to go Les Angles summit — Stage 3
    Pogacar's sprint erases Vingegaard's lead and transfers yellow
    Del Toro delivers a prolonged lead-out pace on the final 1.7 km Les Angles ramp before Pogacar launches in the final 200 metres. The Slovenian crosses the line 2 seconds ahead of Vingegaard. With 10 seconds of stage bonus against 6 for Vingegaard, the 6-second GC gap from Stage 1's TTT is cancelled exactly — the two rivals are level on aggregate time, and Pogacar dons yellow by stage-win countback.
§ 03 · Team-by-team

Who pressed, who missed

UAE Team Emirates XRG
Back-to-back stage wins, GC at +6"

UAE enter the race with a clear plan for both GC and stage wins and execute it over the first two days: Pogačar's fastest finale split in the TTT earns the polka-dot jersey, and del Toro's Montjuïc attack gives UAE the Stage 2 win despite a mechanical delay. Pogačar sits second at +0:06, del Toro fourth at +0:16 — the team holds two GC cards in the top five.

Riders: POGAČAR Tadej, DEL TORO Isaac
Team Visma | Lease a Bike
Yellow jersey, controlling but not dominant

Visma's collective TTT masterclass on Stage 1 puts Vingegaard in yellow from day one. On Stage 2, Jorgenson acts as road captain on the Montjuïc laps and Vingegaard finishes fourth, retaining the lead. The 6-second advantage over Pogačar is modest — Visma will need the mountains to extend it.

Riders: VINGEGAARD Jonas
Red Bull - Bora - Hansgrohe
Solid double stage, Evenepoel on podium

Red Bull-Bora set the third-fastest TTT on Stage 1 and Evenepoel finishes third on Stage 2, earning a 4-second bonus to move to third in GC at +0:15. Both Evenepoel and Lipowitz remain in the top eight overall. The mountain stages will determine whether the team can challenge for victory.

Riders: EVENEPOEL Remco, LIPOWITZ Florian
Netcompany Ineos Cycling Team
TTT disrupted but Ganna impresses

Vauquelin's Stage 1 puncture disrupts Ineos's TTT plan, yet Ganna holds second place individually through pure TT strength. On Stage 2 the team's GC options are less visible as the race shifts to puncheur terrain. Ganna and the team enter France needing to define their overall objectives.

Riders: GANNA Filippo
§ 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders only

How each story played out

🇩🇪 Jonas Vingegaard Team Visma | Lease a Bike
LEADER P1

Vingegaard claims yellow at the first opportunity in Stage 1, attacking his own Visma teammates on the Montjuïc climb to record 21'47'' — the fastest individual time of the day. In Stage 2 he controls from yellow, finishes fourth in the same time as the stage winner, and retains the race lead. After two stages the Dane leads by 6 seconds — a small margin that reflects how tightly balanced this Tour is shaping up.

  • 16 kmStage 1: Solos away from Visma on Montjuïc climb, fastest of the day
  • 168.5 kmStage 2: Finishes fourth same time, retains yellow
🇸🇮 Tadej Pogačar UAE Team Emirates XRG
LEADER P2 +0:06

Pogačar posts the fastest finale split in Stage 1's TTT — taking the polka-dot jersey — but finishes 12 seconds behind Vingegaard. In Stage 2 he controls UAE's Montjuïc strategy and crosses in the same time as del Toro, earning a 6-second time bonus to cut his overall deficit to 6 seconds. He celebrates del Toro's win with a symbolic lift at the finish — UAE's dual-captain approach is working precisely as planned.

  • 16 kmStage 1: Fastest over last 3.7 km, takes polka-dot jersey
  • 168.5 kmStage 2: Sits on del Toro's wheel to control rivals, earns 6-second bonus
🇲🇽 Isaac del Toro UAE Team Emirates XRG
LEADER P4 +0:16

Del Toro plays a measured support role in Stage 1 (sixth on GC at +26'') before delivering UAE's Stage 2 victory despite a mid-stage mechanical. His counterattack through the final Montjuïc corner is executed with remarkable composure for a 22-year-old in just his second Tour. The win makes him only the second Mexican in Tour history to win a stage and moves him to fourth in GC at +0:16.

  • 108 kmStage 2: Mechanical forces roadside wait for replacement bike
  • 167 kmStage 2: Chases Skjelmose then attacks to win the stage
🇧🇪 Remco Evenepoel Red Bull - Bora - Hansgrohe
LEADER P3 +0:15

Evenepoel delivers a textbook opening at the Tour — third-fastest TTT on Stage 1 (+0:19 on Vingegaard) and third in the Stage 2 bunch sprint on Montjuïc, earning a 4-second bonus to move to third overall at +0:15. Red Bull-Bora look capable of contesting the overall. The Belgian will need the mountains to close the remaining gap to Vingegaard.

  • 168.5 kmStage 2: Third across the line, earns 4-second GC bonus
🏳️ Florian Lipowitz Red Bull - Bora - Hansgrohe
LEADER P8 +0:45

Lipowitz started the Tour eighth in GC at +0:35 after the TTT and finishes eighth overall after Stage 2 at +0:45. The German climber concedes 10 seconds to the stage-winner group on the hilly Montjuïc — terrain not ideally suited to his pure climbing strength. He will look to the Pyrénées and Alps to make his GC mark.

🇮🇹 Filippo Ganna Netcompany Ineos Cycling Team
LEADER —

Ganna's Stage 1 TTT is his standout moment — carrying Ineos alone after Vauquelin's puncture to post 21'55'', second-best individual time on the day. The specialist is not expected to contest Stage 2's puncheur finale and is not in the top ten on the stage. His Tour role is likely to be limited to TT stages ahead.

  • 19.6 kmStage 1: Second-best individual TTT time (21'55'') after carrying the team solo
🇳🇱 Mathieu van der Poel Alpecin-Premier Tech
HELPER —

Van der Poel sets an interim TTT best of 22'26'' in Stage 1 and anchors Alpecin-Premier Tech to 11th individually. On Stage 2 he is dropped on the final Montjuïc ascent as the lead group reduces, confirming this Tour's hilly circuit stages are not his preferred hunting ground. He will target sprint and classics-style stage opportunities in the weeks ahead.

  • 19.6 kmStage 1: Sets interim best time of 22'26'' for Alpecin-Premier Tech
  • 162 kmStage 2: Dropped on the final Montjuïc ascent
🇫🇷 Lenny Martinez Bahrain Victorious
LEADER P9 +0:53

Martinez finishes 15th in GC after the TTT (+0:50) and then moves to ninth overall after a solid Stage 2 performance (eighth at +0:03). The young Frenchman punches above Bahrain's TTT weight, gaining three positions across two stages. He enters the Pyrénées as a dark horse for the mountain stages.

  • 168.5 kmStage 2: Eighth in the stage, moves to ninth overall
🇬🇧 Tom Pidcock Pinarello-Q36.5 Pro Cycling Team
LEADER P10 +1:00

Pidcock follows the lead group through Stage 2's Montjuïc laps to finish tenth at +0:03 on the stage, moving to tenth overall at +1:00. His ability to stay with the best on the hilly circuit signals that the British climber has the legs for the mountain stages ahead, where he will target stage wins.

§ 06 · Aftermath

Race in progress — 18 stages remaining after Stage 3.

After Stage 3, Tadej Pogacar leads the Tour de France in yellow — level on aggregate time with Jonas Vingegaard, ahead only on stage-win countback. UAE hold two riders in the top four (Pogacar 1st, del Toro 4th at +0:24), while Evenepoel sits third at +0:23 for Red Bull-Bora. Stage 4 (Carcassonne → Foix, hilly, 182 km) on July 7 offers another terrain that suits the puncheurs and could further separate the GC contenders. Skjelmose's Stage 3 time loss has dramatically altered the secondary GC narrative.
