§ 01 · The stage

Magnier takes the opener and the leader's jersey at Tavira

The 183.5 km opening stage from Vila Real de Santo António to Tavira ran to the expected sprint script along the flat eastern Algarve coast. The breakaway was reeled in and the fast men's teams set up for a bunch finish into Tavira.

Paul Magnier timed his effort best in a crowded gallop, holding off Jordi Meeus by four seconds on the line (with the bonus-second margin included) and Pavel Bittner in third. Jasper Philipsen and Arnaud De Lie led the next wave home at the same time as the bunch leaders. Santiago Mesa crossed the line fifth but was relegated to 157th by the commissaires for irregular sprinting, promoting De Lie into the top five.

With the 10-second winner's time bonus, Magnier moved into the overall lead, four seconds clear of Meeus heading into the Fóia summit finish.

§ 02 · GC impact

Magnier leads on bonus seconds

Time bonuses (10-6-4 for the top three) decided the early GC. Magnier holds the leader's jersey four seconds ahead of Meeus and six ahead of Bittner, with the GC men all bunched in the same time pending the Fóia summit finish on stage 2.

§ 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇫🇷 Paul Magnier Soudal Quick-Step
STAGE HUNTER P1 winner

Magnier delivered Soudal Quick-Step's first win of the Algarve, edging Meeus in the bunch sprint into Tavira and pulling on the leader's jersey via the winner's bonus. It was the first leg of what became a two-stage haul for the young Frenchman and an early statement of his sprint form for the spring.

🇧🇪 Jordi Meeus Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
STAGE HUNTER P2 +0:04

Meeus was the closest challenger to Magnier in the opening sprint, taking second and the runner-up bonus. He would finish second to the same rider again on stage 4, leaving the week as the nearly-man of the Algarve sprints.

🇧🇪 Jasper Philipsen Alpecin - Premier Tech
STAGE HUNTER P4 +0:10

Philipsen was in the mix but could not get on terms with Magnier and Meeus, taking fourth at the same time as the bunch. A solid early-season hit-out rather than a result for the Alpecin sprinter.

🇧🇪 Arnaud De Lie Lotto Intermarché
STAGE HUNTER P5 +0:10

De Lie was promoted to fifth after Santiago Mesa's relegation, finishing in the front group at the same time as the podium. A workmanlike opening sprint for the Lotto Intermarché fast man as he built towards the cobbled classics.
