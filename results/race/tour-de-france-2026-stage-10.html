§ S10 · MOUNTAIN · 167 km

Stage 10: Aurillac → Le Lioran
Tour de France 2026

Tadej Pogačar wins the Bastille Day mountain stage into Le Lioran — his third stage victory at this Tour — after a devastating attack on the Col de Pertus that swept past a lone Richard Carapaz. Remco Evenepoel fights back from difficulty to finish second (+0:32), while Jonas Vingegaard (+0:44), Isaac del Toro (+1:31), and Lenny Martinez (+2:03) all lose significant time, reshaping the overall classification heading into the Alps.

§ 01 · The stage

Pogačar powers to Bastille Day glory at Le Lioran, Carapaz's solo breaks hearts

After the rest day, Stage 10 tackled the volcanic Massif Central — 167 km from Aurillac to a summit finish at Le Lioran, a terrain of shorter, punchier climbs unlike the high Pyrenean cols. A 31-rider breakaway formed in the run-up to the hills, including Thymen Arensman, Mathieu van der Poel, Ben Healy, Alex Baudin, Kévin Vauquelin, Marc Hirschi, and Richard Carapaz. The UAE Team Emirates-led peloton allowed a manageable gap as the break crested the Côte de Pailherols with roughly a minute in hand.

Within the break, Javier Romo, Harold Tejada, and Jordan Jegat pushed ahead; Jegat was unable to maintain the pace, but Romo and Tejada extended to 1:30 over the peloton. On the Col de la Griffoul the chasing group fragmented, and Romo pushed on alone. He reached the summit 35 seconds clear of Valentin Paret-Peintre, Alex Baudin, and Ramses Debruyne, while the UAE peloton was one minute behind. The three chasers were swept up approaching the Côte de Murat, and Romo was eventually caught on the lower slopes of the Puy Mary (Pas de Peyrol).

On the Puy Mary, with 6.9 km of the climb remaining, Richard Carapaz made his defining move. The Ecuadorian built a 20-second lead over the remnants of the break at the summit, then extended to more than one minute on the valley floor heading into the Col de Pertus. His advantage began to shrink on the steep upper slopes of the Pertus. When the gap came down to 45 seconds, Tadej Pogačar launched his attack from the yellow-jersey group. He caught Carapaz just 250 metres from the Col de Pertus summit and immediately powered past the former Olympic champion. The chasing group — Evenepoel, Vingegaard, Seixas, Lipowitz, Ayuso, Skjelmose — trimmed the deficit to 20 seconds as Pogačar crested.

On the Col de Font de Cère, Pogačar extended his lead while Remco Evenepoel suffered and was dropped by the Vingegaard group. The Belgian refused to concede: in the closing kilometres to Le Lioran he fought back, regained the wheels of Vingegaard, Seixas, Lipowitz, Ayuso, and Skjelmose, then hit the front of that group and launched a sprint to claim second place — 32 seconds behind an untouchable Pogačar. Skjelmose and Ayuso also finished in the same group, with Vingegaard seventh at +0:44. Isaac del Toro cracked badly and arrived 1:31 down, a severe blow to his overall ambitions. Richard Carapaz, despite being caught and passed by Pogačar, held on brilliantly to finish 11th at +2:09, well clear of the main bunch.

§ 02 · GC impact

Pogačar tightens grip; del Toro implodes; Evenepoel rises to third.

§ 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇸🇮 Tadej Pogačar UAE Team Emirates XRG
LEADER P1

A commanding Bastille Day display from Pogačar, his third stage victory at the 2026 Tour. UAE Team Emirates controlled the peloton across the early climbs, allowing the break a manageable gap. With Carapaz 45 seconds clear on the Col de Pertus, Pogačar unleashed an acceleration from the yellow-jersey group that caught and passed the Ecuadorian 250 metres from the summit. He then extended his lead on the Col de Font de Cère and held a 32-second advantage at the Le Lioran summit finish. The attack was timed perfectly — measured, explosive, and decisive — underscoring his superiority over the rest of the GC field in the Massif Central mountains.

🇧🇪 Remco Evenepoel Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER P2 +0:32

A rollercoaster stage for Evenepoel that ended positively: his response to a moment of real difficulty shows genuine GC character. He was dropped by the Vingegaard group on the Col de Font de Cère, seemingly set to lose significant time across the board. Instead, the Belgian dug deep, regained the wheels, pushed to the front of the chasing group, and sprinted to second place — 32 seconds behind the stage winner. The result earned him a 6-second time bonus and moved him from fourth to third overall, now +4:06. On a day where del Toro cracked badly, Evenepoel's resilience secured a critical GC position ahead of the Alps.

🇫🇷 Paul Seixas Decathlon CMA CGM Team
LEADER P3 +0:34

A superb Bastille Day for the French hope: third place at Le Lioran, matching the summit finish and earning a 4-second time bonus. Seixas stayed with the Vingegaard group through the critical Col de Font de Cère section, let Evenepoel's late surge go, and finished third — moving up from sixth to fifth in the overall classification at +4:35. He now sits as the best-placed Frenchman in the GC, an encouraging result ahead of what promises to be a decisive Alps block.

🏳️ Florian Lipowitz Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER P4 +0:34

Fourth place and a solid showing for the German climber, who stayed with the Vingegaard group through the most demanding sections. Lipowitz finished level with Seixas in time but takes 4th on countback, rising from seventh to sixth in the overall standings at +4:44. A disciplined GC ride, keeping pace with the best of the rest while teammate Evenepoel fought back ahead of him.

🏳️ Juan Ayuso Lidl-Trek
LEADER P5 +0:38

Fifth place on a mountain stage where Ayuso matched the pace of the Vingegaard group without cracking, losing only 38 seconds to Pogačar on the stage. He benefits from del Toro's implosion to rise one place to fourth in the GC at +4:22, now the highest-placed Lidl-Trek rider overall. Ayuso is building quietly ahead of the Alps.

🇩🇪 Mattias Skjelmose Lidl-Trek
LEADER P6 +0:38

Skjelmose matched the Vingegaard group at +0:38 alongside Ayuso, a solid climb to Le Lioran. He remains in the GC picture, rising from ninth to eighth overall at +5:45 as del Toro's collapse shuffled the classification. The Dane will be looking to claw back more time in the Alps.

🇩🇪 Jonas Vingegaard Team Visma | Lease a Bike
LEADER P7 +0:44

A concerning day for Vingegaard: he led the chase group after Pogačar's attack but lost 44 seconds to the yellow jersey — 54 seconds including time bonuses — extending his GC deficit from 2:42 to 3:36. He could not respond to Evenepoel's late sprint for second, suggesting his climbing legs are not yet at Pogačar's level at this Tour. The 10-stage gap of +3:36 is substantial, though the Alps remain ahead. Visma will need a more aggressive strategy if they are to challenge for overall victory.

🇲🇽 Isaac del Toro UAE Team Emirates XRG
LEADER P8 +1:31

A stage to forget for the young Mexican, the most significant GC reversal of the day. Del Toro had been third overall heading into Stage 10 but cracked badly in the final mountain section, finishing 1:31 behind Pogačar. Combined with the stage bonus loss, he surrendered 1:41 to the yellow jersey in one afternoon, plummeting from third to seventh overall at +5:08. For a rider who had looked secure in the top three, it is a harsh lesson in Tour de France mountain racing; how he responds in the Alps will define his 2026 campaign.

🏳️ Richard Carapaz EF Education - EasyPost
STAGE HUNTER P11 +2:09

Carapaz was the heroic protagonist of Stage 10, animating the race with a solo attack on the Puy Mary (Pas de Peyrol) with 6.9 km of the climb remaining. He crested the summit with a 20-second lead and extended to over a minute on the valley floor, carrying that advantage through the Col de Pertus until Pogačar hunted him down 250 metres from the summit. Caught and dropped, Carapaz refused to collapse, finishing 11th at +2:09 — comfortably ahead of all remaining chasers. It was an EF Education - EasyPost moment of genuine ambition on Bastille Day, ultimately eclipsed only by the stage winner's extraordinary power.

🏳️ Tom Pidcock Pinarello-Q36.5 Pro Cycling Team
STAGE HUNTER P9 +1:59

Pidcock rode in a group behind the main GC battle, finishing ninth at +1:59. He was not a factor in the key exchanges — the race was decided between Pogačar, Evenepoel, and the Vingegaard group — but he climbed competently in the Massif Central. His GC position drops to tenth at +11:49, essentially out of GC contention, but the upcoming stages may offer opportunities for another stage-hunter result.

🇫🇷 Lenny Martinez Bahrain Victorious
LEADER P10 +2:03

A difficult day for Martinez, who had been eighth in the GC at +4:21. He finished in a group 2:03 behind the stage winner, conceding 2:13 to Pogačar including the bonus; he slides to ninth overall at +6:34. The home rider faces a significant climb back if he is to feature in the GC picture, needing to find time in the Alps.
