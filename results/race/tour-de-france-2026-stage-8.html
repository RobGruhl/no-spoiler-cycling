§ 01 · The stage

Merlier storms back from lost position for double in Bergerac

Stage 8 runs 180.4km through the Dordogne wine country from Périgueux to Bergerac, the third sprint opportunity of the 2026 Tour. Liam Slock, Thibault Guernalec, and Jakub Otruba form the day's breakaway, building a maximum lead of 2 minutes as the peloton rolls through the Dordogne valleys at a controlled tempo, sprint teams content to let the escape run.

The Côte du Buisson-de-Cadouin, a category 4 climb roughly 60km from the finish, splinters the break. Slock accelerates alone over the summit, while Guernalec and Otruba fade back to the bunch. The Belgian rider presses on solo into the Bergerac approach, and with 10km remaining still holds a lead of more than 1 minute over the peloton, the sprint teams now urgently ramping up the tempo.

By 4km to go, Slock's advantage has been halved. He is caught with just 1.3km remaining, the peloton swarming around him as the medieval Bergerac streets come into view. In the sprint that follows, Tim Merlier finds himself in a seemingly impossible position — boxed in with the field streaming past — but produces what CyclingStage described as 'a stunning comeback from what looked like a lost position,' threading through gaps in the final 200m to take his second stage win in as many days. Biniam Girmay finishes second for NSN Cycling Team, Olav Kooij third for Decathlon CMA CGM Team. Tadej Pogačar crosses safely in the bunch, his yellow jersey never threatened.

§ 02 · GC impact

GC unchanged — Pogačar retains yellow with all contenders safe in the bunch.

Stage 8 is a pure sprint day with no GC consequence. All general classification contenders finished safely in the peloton. § 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇧🇪 Tim Merlier Soudal Quick-Step
STAGE HUNTER P1

Back-to-back stage wins for the Soudal Quick-Step sprinter — Merlier's third Tour stage victory and a demonstration of composure under pressure. After winning on the wide Garonne boulevard in Bordeaux on Stage 7, he delivers again on Bergerac's tighter medieval-street approach. Found himself boxed in during the chaotic final sprint but forced a gap in the final 200m to claim what onlookers described as a stunning comeback win. His ability to sprint from difficult positions sets him apart from the other pure speed men in this race.

🇪🇷 Biniam Girmay NSN Cycling Team
STAGE HUNTER P2 s.t.

Girmay takes second for NSN Cycling Team in Bergerac, his most consistent week of sprint finishes at the Tour. Unable to match Merlier's late acceleration after the Belgian found space in the chaotic final metres, but a strong result on a stage that suits Girmay's punchy sprint. With two top-3 finishes in as many flat stages, he is the second-best sprinter of this Tour's opening week.

🇳🇱 Olav Kooij Decathlon CMA CGM Team
STAGE HUNTER P3 s.t.

Kooij adds a third-place to his Stage 5 win in Pau, establishing himself as one of the top sprinters at this Tour. Decathlon CMA CGM deliver him well-positioned in the hectic Bergerac finale, and the Dutchman holds his nerve in the tight streets to pip Philipsen for the final podium spot.

🇧🇪 Jasper Philipsen Alpecin-Premier Tech
STAGE HUNTER P4 s.t.

A second consecutive fourth place for Philipsen, who is failing to convert Alpecin-Premier Tech's leadout into top-3 results so far in this edition. He had been looking to improve on Stage 7's 5th place, but Merlier, Girmay, and Kooij all edge him out again. The Belgian faces increasing pressure as the sprinting windows narrow heading into the hillier second week.
