§ 01 · The stage

Ferguson takes the sprint in Vila-Real as Vollering holds the lead

The 115.5 km circuit around Vila-Real packed its climbing into the first half before roughly 50 km of mostly downhill and flat roads back to the line — a profile that pointed to a sprint if the fast finishers survived the hills. They did, and the day came down to a group gallop.

Movistar's 19-year-old Cat Ferguson timed the finish best, winning ahead of Letizia Paternoster (Liv AlUla Jayco) and Letizia Borghesi (AG Insurance-Soudal). The tight time spread among the leaders confirmed a bunch finish rather than a successful long break. Demi Vollering came in safely with the front of the race and retained the overall lead heading into the scheduled queen stage.

§ 02 · GC impact

No change at the top — Vollering still leads

Ferguson's stage win came with the time bonus but from well down on GC, so the overall order at the front was unchanged: Vollering led from Squiban (+0:56) and Chladoňová (+0:58), with Chladoňová still in the youth jersey. The flat finish kept the GC contenders together and left the race to be settled on the mountains — or so it seemed before the weather intervened.
