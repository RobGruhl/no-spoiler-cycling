§ 01 · The stage

UAE's day from hell, Silva takes the stage

221 km Burgas to Veliko Tarnovo. Flat opening 100 km, then two punchy climbing loops. The decisive event of the day arrived on the rain-soaked descent into Veliko Tarnovo with ~21-23 km to go: a mass pile-up that took out four UAE Team Emirates-XRG riders simultaneously — Jay Vine (broken elbow + concussion, immediately withdrawn), Marc Soler (fractured pelvis), Adam Yates (heavy abrasions, head laceration, delayed concussive symptoms diagnosed next morning), Baroncini (joined the injury list shortly after).

At the front, the climbing thinned the bunch to a small group; Silva took the reduced sprint from Stork and Ciccone, with Pellizzari completing the top five.

§ 02 · GC impact

UAE GC plan in ruins

The pre-race UAE GC plan dissolved on a single descent. With Vine, Soler, Yates, and Baroncini all out within hours, UAE's Giro became a stage-hunting operation rather than a GC bid. Narváez would deliver a stage win two days later on Stage 4.
