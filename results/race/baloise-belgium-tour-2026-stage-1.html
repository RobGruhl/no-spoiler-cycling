§ 01 · The stage

Girmay edges the bunch sprint to grab the first leader's jersey

The 95th Baloise Belgium Tour began with a 188.3 km circuit-heavy day around Scherpenheuvel-Zichem, a route punchy enough to thin the field but always likely to come down to the fast men. The breakaway of the day was reeled in inside the closing laps and the sprinters' teams set up the finale. In a tight, slightly uphill-tinged dash Biniam Girmay timed his effort best, holding off Tim Merlier and Max Kanter. Søren Wærenskjold, Emilien Jeannière and the other quick finishers were packed into the same second behind. With time bonuses, Girmay took the first overall lead of the race, two seconds clear of Bart Kortleve.

§ 02 · GC impact

Girmay into the leader's jersey on bonuses

Girmay's stage win plus the 10-second winner's bonus put him in the race lead, two seconds ahead of Bart Kortleve (Metec-SOLARWATT) and three over Robbe Mellaerts. Tim Merlier sat fourth at four seconds, with the GC tightly bunched among the sprinters and puncheurs ahead of the harder days to come.
