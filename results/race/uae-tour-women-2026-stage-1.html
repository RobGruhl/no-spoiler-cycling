§ 01 · The stage

Wiebes wins the opener and pulls on the first red jersey

The 111 km run from Al Mirfa to Madinat Zayed across the desert produced exactly the flat, controlled stage the sprinters wanted, with no breakaway given meaningful freedom on the open roads. The finish was a fast, congested affair as competing lead-out trains swamped the final kilometre and positioning became chaotic on the run-in.

Lorena Wiebes, kept near the front all day by SD Worx - Protime, found a gap along the barriers and surged through to take the win. Lara Gillespie was second for the home UAE Team ADQ squad with Zoe Bäckstedt third for Canyon-SRAM. The win, plus bonus seconds, gave Wiebes the first red leader's jersey of the race.

§ 02 · GC impact

Wiebes in red after the opening sprint

With time bonuses on a stage that finished together, Wiebes took the red jersey. The GC sat tightly bunched among the sprinters and finishers, with the climbers biding their time for the Jebel Hafeet summit finish on stage 4. Gillespie sat second on bonuses, Bäckstedt third.
