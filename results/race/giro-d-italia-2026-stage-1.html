§ 01 · The stage

Magnier takes Stage 1 and the pink

147 km along the Bulgarian Black Sea coast from Nessebar to Burgas, finishing on two laps of a short city circuit. A late crash inside the final 3 km meant same-time bunch credit for GC; the front of the sprint was contested clean. Magnier — Soudal's 22-year-old French sprinter — claimed his first Grand Tour stage and the first maglia rosa of the 2026 Giro.

§ 02 · GC impact

Magnier in pink

First leader's jersey to Magnier on time bonuses. The opening trio of stages in Bulgaria were always going to be a holding period for GC; the climbing returned with Stage 7.

§ 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇫🇷 Paul Magnier Soudal Quick-Step
STAGE HUNTER P1

Opened the 2026 Giro with a clean bunch-sprint win in Burgas on the Bulgarian Grande Partenza, pulling on the first maglia rosa of the edition. The 21-year-old Soudal Quick-Step fast man timed the gallop perfectly to announce himself as the sprint to beat.

🇮🇹 Jonathan Milan Lidl-Trek
SPRINTER P4 s.t.

Fourth in the Giro opener — solid but Lidl-Trek's lead-out couldn't quite get Milan to the front of the sprint train. The week ahead had three more flat stages for him to convert.
