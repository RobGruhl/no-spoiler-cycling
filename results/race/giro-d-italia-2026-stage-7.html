Stage 7: Formia → Blockhaus
Giro d'Italia 2026

Jonas Vingegaard won the Giro's first mountain finish at Blockhaus, soloing the final 7 km to take 13 seconds over Felix Gall — but Afonso Eulálio survived the day to retain the pink jersey, with his Stage 6 cushion halved. Hindley led the next chase 1:02 back, edging Pellizzari and O'Connor in a 3-way sprint. Vingegaard is now the clear GC threat, but Eulálio still leads.

§ 01 · The stage

Vingegaard stakes his Giro claim at Blockhaus — but Eulálio holds pink

245 km, the longest stage of the Giro, ~4,500 m of climbing weighted to the second half. The day's break (Milan, Sevilla, Zukowski, van der Lee, Naberman) was a points-jersey skirmish more than a GC threat; Milan dropped back after the intermediate sprint, Sevilla kept gathering KOM points until Vingegaard later jeopardised the blue jersey too.

Visma took control as the race approached the 13 km Blockhaus climb. Piganzoli and Kuss launched Vingegaard, who attacked with 7 km to go. Eulálio was dropped seconds before the move — only Pellizzari could initially follow, with Gall close behind. Pellizzari cracked at 5 km to go and Gall came across to be the lone chaser, never closing inside 13 seconds.

Vingegaard's first Grand Tour stage outside the Tour de France. Hindley won the 3-way sprint for third over Pellizzari and O'Connor 1:02 back. Crucially, Eulálio crossed about three minutes down — he lost half his lead but kept the maglia rosa.

§ 02 · GC impact

Eulálio still in pink, Vingegaard now the clear chaser

Eulálio retains the maglia rosa after a strong defensive ride from the Stage 6 breakaway leader. Vingegaard is now the principal GC threat, with Gall close behind. Pellizzari moved further into the GC top six. Hindley and O'Connor stayed in the conversation. The race's GC tension has reset: not yet 'Vingegaard's to lose,' but the Dane is closer to pink than anyone outside Eulálio's own breakaway group from Stage 6.

Storylines from the stage

🇩🇪 Jonas Vingegaard Team Visma | Lease a Bike
LEADER P1

Won the 244 km Blockhaus queen stage — the longest of the Giro — attacking on the 13.6 km final climb and taking 13 seconds on Felix Gall, the only rider able to follow, while the rest of the GC field shipped a minute or more. The GC statement of the first week, even though Afonso Eulálio's breakaway buffer kept the maglia rosa out of reach for now.

🇮🇹 Giulio Pellizzari Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER P4 +1:05

Followed Vingegaard's attack at 7 km to go — the only rider to initially stay on the Dane's wheel. Cracked at ~5 km to go after 'going above what he could,' losing contact with Gall on the steepest section. Recovered to finish 4th in the 3-way sprint behind Hindley. The day Pellizzari's stage-race credibility cleared from 'top-five at Tirreno' to 'Grand Tour mountain-stage top-five on home Italian roads' — Red Bull-Bora's GC plan around him has its strongest data point yet.

🇮🇹 Jonathan Milan Lidl-Trek
STAGE HUNTER —

In the early breakaway, taking 12 maglia ciclamino points at the intermediate sprint before dropping back to the bunch. A points-jersey play, not a stage-result one — the right use of a queen-mountain stage for the race's lead sprinter.
