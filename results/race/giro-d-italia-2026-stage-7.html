Stage 7: Formia → Blockhaus
Giro d'Italia 2026

Jonas Vingegaard took the Giro's first mountain finish — the legendary Blockhaus, 244 km, ~4,500 m climbing — 13 seconds clear of Felix Gall and crushing the rest of the GC field. Pellizzari held fourth at +1:05, the day's most encouraging young-Italian-climber performance. The Giro is now Vingegaard's race to lose.

§ 01 · The stage

Vingegaard's Giro claim staked at Blockhaus

244 km, the longest stage of the Giro. ~4,500 m of climbing, bulk in the second half. Pre-final climbs (Rionero Sannitico, Roccaraso KOM) thinned the bunch through the long middle phase; the GC selection happened on the final 13.6 km Blockhaus climb, with its very steep middle section.

Vingegaard rode the kind of solo from the front Paris-Nice Stage 5 had previewed — attack went earlier than the rivals expected, gap opened on the steepest section, never closed. Gall did the best chase but couldn't get inside 13 seconds. Hindley and Pellizzari led the next chase a minute back.

The career-rebooting Visma GC year now has a Giro stage win + presumed leader's jersey to add to the Paris-Nice overall. Vingegaard's first Grand Tour stage outside of a Tour de France.

§ 02 · GC impact

Vingegaard into pink, big margin

Stage win plus more than a minute on every GC threat except Gall, who's now Vingegaard's principal challenger for the overall.
