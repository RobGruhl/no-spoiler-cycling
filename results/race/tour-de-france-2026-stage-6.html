Stage 6: Pau → Gavarnie-Gèdre
Tour de France 2026

Tadej Pogačar attacks solo on the Col du Tourmalet, rides clear of all rivals for a dominant solo victory and reclaims the yellow jersey from Torstein Træen. § 01 · The stage

Pogačar rides alone over the Tourmalet to reclaim the maillot jaune

Stage 6 covers 186.2 km from Pau through the heart of the High Pyrenees: the Col d'Aspin (HC) and the legendary Col du Tourmalet (HC, 17.1 km) before a final Cat-2 drag up to the cliff-backed cirque of Gavarnie-Gèdre. Victor Campenaerts attacks from the gun, with Huub Artz (Lotto Intermarché) and green jersey Mads Pedersen quickly bridging across. The trio builds a lead of 1 min 30s before Artz eases off at 35 km; Campenaerts and Pedersen press on, with Pedersen claiming maximum points at the intermediate sprint. The peloton, driven by UAE Team Emirates, reels in the duo with around 122 km remaining, at which point Ben O'Connor (Jayco–AlUla) slips clear alone, tackling the Aspin with a 60-second advantage. UAE maintain relentless pressure and O'Connor is caught just past the summit, with Lenny Martinez sweeping up the mountain points at the top of the Aspin.

On the lower slopes of the Tourmalet, the peloton is whittled down rapidly. Yellow jersey holder Torstein Træen, Thymen Arensman and Matteo Jorgenson lose contact well before the final 10 km of the iconic HC climb. UAE set a brutal pace at the front; Isaac del Toro moves to the head of the group inside the final 5 km of the Tourmalet with only Jonas Vingegaard, Paul Seixas and Tadej Pogačar able to follow. That group fragments almost immediately — del Toro and Pogačar surge clear, and moments later Pogačar accelerates solo away from his own teammate, leaving Vingegaard grasping in his wake.

Pogačar crests the Tourmalet summit 30 seconds ahead of Vingegaard, with the chasers — Seixas, del Toro, Lipowitz and the emerging group of Evenepoel, Ayuso, Skjelmose and Martinez — more than a minute behind. On the descent Pogačar extends his lead further, while the chase group consolidates: Evenepoel, Ayuso, Skjelmose and Martinez bridge across to del Toro and Seixas. Simultaneously, Træen crashes after touching wheels with Uno-X teammate Anders Johannessen; he remounts and finishes the stage, but the incident costs him the bulk of his GC lead. On the final Cat-2 climb to Gavarnie-Gèdre, Pogačar continues to pull away while Vingegaard just manages to hold off the chasers for second place.

Pogačar wins solo in 4 h 32' 07'', the first reigning world champion to conquer the Tourmalet. Vingegaard takes second at 2:38; del Toro out-sprints Evenepoel and the group for third at 2:57. Træen eventually rolls in 29 min 55s down, effectively ending his overall challenge. The yellow jersey returns to Pogačar — his third in as many Tours — with a transformed GC picture heading into the rest-day tomorrow and the Massif Central beyond.

§ 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇸🇮 Tadej Pogačar UAE Team Emirates XRG
LEADER P1

Pogačar enters Stage 6 lying 7:53 behind yellow jersey Træen after the sprint interlude in Pau, knowing the Tourmalet is designed for him. UAE set the tempo from the Aspin onwards, perfectly pacing del Toro for the mountain train. With 5 km remaining on the Tourmalet, del Toro pushes the pace at the front, drawing out Vingegaard; Pogačar then uncorks his attack, a surge so devastating that even his own teammate cannot respond. He crests the summit 30 seconds clear, descends with precision, and climbs the final Cat-2 ramp to Gavarnie still accelerating — winning solo and reclaiming yellow. As reigning world champion, he is the first to conquer the Tourmalet in the rainbow jersey.

🇩🇪 Jonas Vingegaard Team Visma | Lease a Bike
LEADER P2 +2:38

Vingegaard enters Stage 6 level on GC with Pogačar (both at +7:53 to Træen) but without UAE's depth for mountain control. He follows del Toro's acceleration at 5 km to go on the Tourmalet but cannot hold Pogačar's surge and finishes the Tourmalet 30 seconds behind. On the descent and final climb to Gavarnie he manages to hold off the chase group, limiting the damage to 2:38 on the day. He now trails Pogačar by 2:42 in GC. 'I still believe in myself, I still believe my legs would get better throughout the race. The fight is not over,' he says at the finish.

🇲🇽 Isaac del Toro UAE Team Emirates XRG
LEADER P3 +2:57

Del Toro is UAE's key engine on the Tourmalet, moving to the front of the group inside the final 5 km as part of the plan to isolate Pogačar's rivals. The 22-year-old can hold the pace himself until Pogačar accelerates — at which point only Vingegaard briefly follows before del Toro is dropped. He outsprints the chase group (Evenepoel, Seixas, Lipowitz, Ayuso, Skjelmose, Martinez) on the Gavarnie finish ramp for third place, earning bonus seconds that lift him from 8th to 3rd in GC at +3:27. A major step forward in his debut Tour de France.

🇧🇪 Remco Evenepoel Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER P4 s.t.

Evenepoel is dropped from the Tourmalet lead group before the final kilometres but recovers on the descent to bridge across to del Toro's chase group alongside Ayuso, Skjelmose and Martinez. On the final climb to Gavarnie he cannot match del Toro's sprint and takes 4th, losing s.t. vs the podium group. He moves from 7th to 4th in GC at +3:30 after Træen's collapse.

🇫🇷 Paul Seixas Decathlon CMA CGM Team
LEADER P5 s.t.

Seixas has an exceptional stage. He follows the decisive move at 5 km from the Tourmalet summit alongside Vingegaard — the 19-year-old matching the two-time Tour winner — and despite being overtaken by Evenepoel's bridging group on the descent, he finishes in the same group at +2:57. Reuters notes that Paret-Peintre and Riccitello, his Decathlon teammates, were dropped on the Tourmalet while Seixas survived. He moves from 10th to 6th in GC at +3:55.

🏳️ Florian Lipowitz Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER P6 s.t.

Lipowitz is one of seven riders to finish in the same group as del Toro at +2:57. He matches a strong GC field on the Tourmalet and finishes 6th on the stage. The time gaps from Træen's collapse see him move from 11th to 7th in GC at +4:00.

🏳️ Juan Ayuso Lidl-Trek
LEADER P7 s.t.

Ayuso drops from the Tourmalet lead group but, like Evenepoel, bridges back to the del Toro chase on the descent. He finishes 7th in the sprint at +2:57. Despite being slightly further back in the stage than del Toro, the time swings from Træen's disaster lift him from 9th to 5th in GC at +3:34 — his best GC position of the race so far.

🇩🇪 Mattias Skjelmose Lidl-Trek
LEADER P8 s.t.

Skjelmose finishes 8th in the del Toro group at +2:57, a strong result that lifts him from 18th to 9th in GC at +4:57 after Træen's collapse shakes the standings. The Dane was in solid form on both major climbs.

🇫🇷 Lenny Martinez Bahrain Victorious
LEADER P9 +3:02

Martinez sweeps up the King of the Mountains points on the Col d'Aspin (his early move on the descent of the Aspin earns him the points). He then finishes 9th on the stage at +3:02 in the del Toro chase group, moving from 12th to 8th in GC at +4:21. The most impressive climber outside the UAE–Visma axis on the day.

🇨🇴 Egan Bernal INEOS Grenadiers
LEADER P11 +4:47

Bernal finishes 11th on the stage at +4:47, clear of the large grupetto groups but behind the Kuss group. His efforts lift him to 10th in GC at +9:12 as riders above him capitulate. It is the best GC moment of his Pyrenean stage and keeps him in touching distance of the top 10 heading into the Massif Central.

🏳️ Tom Pidcock Pinarello-Q36.5 Pro Cycling Team
LEADER P15 +8:18

Pidcock is among the riders who drop back on the Tourmalet, finishing 15th on the stage at +8:18 in a large group. He loses ground in GC — from 15th at +9:15 pre-stage to around 12th at +9:50 as the standings compact following Træen's crash. The Tourmalet was a clear stumbling block for the Pinarello-Q36.5 leader.

🏳️ Richard Carapaz EF Education - EasyPost
LEADER P23 +8:51

Carapaz finishes 23rd on the stage at +8:51, losing significant time versus the GC contenders on the Tourmalet. He falls to approximately 15th in GC at +10:46. A difficult day for the Ecuadorian, who had been the most consistent EF finisher in the mountain stages until now.

🇳🇱 Thymen Arensman Netcompany-INEOS Cycling Team
DOMESTIQUE P29 +16:50

Arensman is one of the first GC riders to crack, dropped from the Træen group well before the final 10 km of the Tourmalet. He finishes 29th on the stage at +16:50 — a loss of more than 6 minutes beyond the main GC groups. Already outside the top 20 in GC before the stage, he drops further down overall. INEOS's race-within-a-race continues to trend backwards.

🇩🇪 Mads Pedersen Lidl-Trek
STAGE HUNTER —

Pedersen attacks from the gun with Victor Campenaerts and Huub Artz, racing in the early break as green jersey holder to accumulate sprint points. He wins the intermediate sprint (ahead of Campenaerts, Kanter, Philipsen, Girmay), consolidating his lead in the points classification. As the stage turns mountainous he falls back into the sprinter grupetto with Philipsen and others, eventually finishing well outside the GC-relevant groups but managing the time cut. A tactical success for his green jersey defence.

§ 05 · Stage incidents

Crashes, abandons

CRASH · 175 km

Yellow jersey Torstein Træen crashes on the descent from the Col du Tourmalet after touching wheels with Uno-X Mobility teammate Anders Johannessen. Træen remounts and finishes the stage but loses nearly 30 minutes, ending his overall challenge. He drops from the race lead to well outside the top 10 in GC.
