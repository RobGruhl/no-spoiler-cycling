Stage 11: Vichy → Nevers
Tour de France 2026

Søren Wærenskjold wins Stage 11 of the Tour de France with a long-range sprint in Nevers — the fastest stage in Tour history at 50.9 km/h — after Jasper Philipsen is relegated for impeding Pavel Bittner. Tadej Pogačar retains the yellow jersey and the general classification order is unchanged.

§ 01 · The stage

Wærenskjold's long-range lightning strike sets all-time Tour speed record.

Stage 11 delivered the relief the peloton needed after the Bastille Day fireworks — 161.3 km of almost pan-flat road from Vichy to Nevers along the Loire valley, with favourable tailwinds that would ultimately propel the bunch to a record average of 50.9 km/h, comfortably surpassing Mario Cipollini's 1999 benchmark of 50.3 km/h set into Blois.

A four-man breakaway of Julian Alaphilippe, Nelson Oliveira, Anthon Charmig and Mathis Le Berre formed after 13 kilometres. The sprint squads — Decathlon, UAE, Alpecin and Uno-X — were never inclined to let the move go far; the group reached a maximum lead of 1:30. Alaphilippe, the former world champion who has powered many ambitious escapes in his career, was the first to be shed, dropping from the break on the Côte de Billy-Chévannes. The remaining three were swept up inside the final 6 km as the peloton ramped up for the sprint.

In the final 5 km a sprint team stand-off developed, teams jockeying for position but reluctant to burn riders too early. With around 250 metres remaining, Cees Bol (Decathlon CMA CGM) launched an early acceleration to lead out Kooij, opening a gap along the right-hand barriers. Wærenskjold, who had been further back in the bunch, read the move instantly — he threaded through the gap in Bol's slipstream, held his sprint back for a fraction of a second, and then unleashed a devastating effort that carried him clear of Kooij and Philipsen before they could respond. 'I saw Cees Bol had a gap and I tried to sprint up to him and hold a little bit back to go from his wheel,' Wærenskjold explained. 'Then I saw there were 250 to go. I was just waiting for the same thing to happen when Merlier passed me, but it didn't happen this time.'

Jasper Philipsen crossed the line third but was relegated to 19th place by the jury for moving on Pavel Bittner during the sprint, promoting Milan Fretin (Cofidis) to the podium. The general classification is entirely unchanged: Pogačar leads by 3:36 from Vingegaard, with Evenepoel a further 30 seconds back in third.

§ 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇳🇴 Søren Wærenskjold Uno-X Mobility
STAGE HUNTER P1

An opportunistic and tactically brilliant stage win from the 24-year-old Norwegian — his first ever Tour de France stage victory and only the second in Uno-X Mobility's history. Wærenskjold was well-placed but not on the wheel he wanted in the chaotic final 5 km. When Cees Bol opened a gap along the barriers with 250 m to go in a lead-out for Kooij, Wærenskjold reacted immediately, slotted onto Bol's wheel, waited until he had the maximum momentum advantage, and then powered through at a speed even the fastest pure sprinters couldn't match. 'It means everything, it's my biggest win so far,' he said afterwards. 'Like I said when I came here, there are two or three guys here who are faster, but if I'm lucky and have a good sprint like today then it's possible.' The victory was particularly sweet coming one week after he had been beaten to second by Tim Merlier on Stage 7 into Bordeaux.

🇳🇱 Olav Kooij Decathlon CMA CGM Team
STAGE HUNTER P2 s.t.

Kooij had arguably the best lead-out of the sprint when Cees Bol opened the right-side lane along the barriers, but Wærenskjold was in the gap before Kooij could capitalise. The Dutchman, who won Stage 5 in Pau, edged Philipsen (later relegated) for the unofficial second place but could not bridge to Wærenskjold's early jump. A second-place finish maintained his sprinting form.

🇧🇪 Jasper Philipsen Alpecin-Premier Tech
STAGE HUNTER INCIDENT s.t.

Philipsen crossed the finish line in third place but was relegated to 19th by the race jury for impeding Pavel Bittner during the sprint. His relegation promoted Milan Fretin to the podium. It was a frustrating outcome for the Belgian who had been competitive throughout the sprint stages.

⚠ relegated
🇪🇷 Biniam Girmay NSN Cycling Team
STAGE HUNTER P5 s.t.

Girmay, the points classification leader, contested the sprint and took fifth place. He had already won the intermediate sprint points earlier in the stage and continued his consistent showing in the bunch finishes.

🇩🇪 Mads Pedersen Lidl-Trek
STAGE HUNTER P10 s.t.

Pedersen came in tenth, contributing to Lidl-Trek's points-classification battle. The former world champion was active at the intermediate sprint but could not replicate a high placing at the finish in the deeply contested bunch sprint.
