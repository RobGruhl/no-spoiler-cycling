§ 01 · The race in 90 seconds

Van der Poel takes the E3 rehearsal for Flanders

OPENINGFrom Harelbeke, the conventional E3 sequence — control through the early sectors, building tension for the final climbing run. The Tour of Flanders was nine days away and the cobbled set-piece favourites used E3 as a deliberate dress rehearsal.

UNFOLDSVan der Poel went late on the canonical run-up sequence — Paterberg and Oude Kwaremont, the same combination he would face at the Ronde — and forged clear with a gap of about 45 seconds at one point. The chasers stabilised around 30 seconds, then tightened to 28 in the closing kilometres.

DECIDEDStrand Hagenes — Visma's 23-year-old Norwegian Classics prospect — bridged out of the chase group with Vermeersch and rode the final kilometres in pursuit. The gap held; Van der Poel took the line solo.

FINALEThree seconds at the finish; the win cleanly Van der Poel's. Strand Hagenes's solo-for-second is the kind of result that ages well — the Visma succession plan beneath Van Aert and Brennan starting to show its second tier.

The Flanders rehearsal goes to plan

Van der Poel used E3 the way the Belgians have always used it: as a course-specific test for the Ronde. Won solo with the exact climbing-sequence attack he'd attempt on April 5. The Ronde would not go the same way.
