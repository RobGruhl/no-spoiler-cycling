§ 01 · The stage

Del Toro conquers Montjuïc despite mechanical nightmare to give UAE back-to-back stage wins.

Stage 2 of the 2026 Tour de France follows the 168.5 km Catalan coast from Tarragona back to Barcelona, finishing on three laps of the Montjuïc circuit. Frank van den Broek, Alex Molenaar, and Felix Engelhardt escape in the opening kilometres and build a lead of over three minutes. The gap falls to 25 seconds on the Côte de Begues, where Molenaar wins the KOM sprint at the summit before van den Broek loses contact. The remaining two survivors cling on for dozens of kilometres before the peloton catches them entering the Montjuïc circuit.

With approximately 60 km remaining, Isaac del Toro suffers a mechanical problem. A radio miscommunication leaves the 22-year-old Mexican waiting by the roadside for a replacement bike — a potential race-ending delay. But del Toro shows his class, needing fewer than 10 km to fully reintegrate into the peloton and immediately resuming a central role in UAE's race plan.

As the peloton is gradually whittled down on the Montjuïc circuit, UAE control the race methodically. Brandon McNulty sets a fierce tempo during the first ascent of the Côte du Château de Montjuïc, Pogačar and three teammates take command during the second lap, and Visma respond through Matteo Jorgenson pushing on the penultimate climb. Mathieu van der Poel is among those dropped on the final ascent, leaving only around 15 riders at the front over the summit.

On the decisive final lap, Mattias Skjelmose launches an acceleration but del Toro immediately responds, reeling the Dane in. Carrying remarkable momentum through a corner, del Toro opens a gap nobody can close. Pogačar tracks his teammate to the line, content to let del Toro win while watching to ensure no one bridges across. Del Toro takes the victory — his first Tour de France stage win in just his second Tour appearance — with Pogačar second, Evenepoel third, and Vingegaard fourth, the Dane retaining the yellow jersey.

§ 02 · GC impact

Vingegaard retains yellow; Pogačar cuts deficit to 6 seconds.

Jonas Vingegaard (Team Visma | Lease a Bike) retains the yellow jersey. Tadej Pogačar (UAE Team Emirates XRG) moves to second at +0:06 after earning the 6-second stage time bonus for finishing second — cutting 6 seconds from the opening-day TTT gap. Remco Evenepoel (Red Bull-Bora-Hansgrohe) jumps to third at +0:15 courtesy of the 4-second bonus. Isaac del Toro (UAE) rises to fourth at +0:16, gaining 10 seconds via the stage-win bonus. Juan Ayuso (Lidl-Trek) joint-fifth at +0:19. Mattias Skjelmose (Lidl-Trek) joint-fifth at +0:19 — both Lidl-Trek riders carried the same TTT deficit and lost the same 3 seconds in Stage 2. Paul Seixas (Decathlon CMA CGM) seventh at +0:42. Romain Grégoire (Groupama-FDJ) eighth at +0:44. Florian Lipowitz (Red Bull-Bora) ninth at +0:45. Lenny Martinez (Bahrain Victorious) tenth at +0:53. Tom Pidcock (Pinarello-Q36.5) eleventh at +1:00.

§ 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇲🇽 Isaac del Toro UAE Team Emirates XRG
LEADER P1

Del Toro delivers one of the stage's most dramatic arcs: he suffers a mechanical issue near km 108, endures a roadside wait for a replacement bike due to a radio miscommunication, and still re-enters the decisive group in time for the Montjuïc finale. He reads the final ascent perfectly — chasing down Skjelmose's acceleration before counterattacking through a corner with speed no one can match. Pogačar tucks in behind but does not contest, allowing his teammate the victory. Del Toro becomes only the second Mexican to win a Tour de France stage, following Raul Alcala in 1986 — and he does it in just his second Tour appearance. GC moves to fourth at +0:16 after absorbing the 10-second stage bonus.

🇸🇮 Tadej Pogačar UAE Team Emirates XRG
LEADER P2 +0:00

Pogačar orchestrates UAE's stage-management brilliantly, using McNulty and teammates to control the Montjuïc laps before del Toro makes his race-winning move. The Slovenian follows his teammate to the line, keeps rivals behind him, and earns a 6-second time bonus — cutting his overall deficit to Vingegaard from 12 seconds to 6. He lifts del Toro at the finish line in a celebration that underlines UAE's collective confidence.

🇧🇪 Remco Evenepoel Red Bull - Bora - Hansgrohe
LEADER P3 +0:00

Evenepoel survives the Montjuïc circuit selection comfortably and earns the 4-second stage time bonus for third place, moving up to third in the GC at +0:15. He finishes just behind the UAE tandem and ahead of Vingegaard — a strong statement from Red Bull-Bora that their GC ambitions are intact despite the TTT gap. The Belgian punches his ticket into the top three overall.

🇩🇪 Jonas Vingegaard Team Visma | Lease a Bike
LEADER P4 +0:00

Vingegaard controls the stage from yellow jersey, keeping Visma's Matteo Jorgenson near the front during the Montjuïc laps. He finishes fourth — same time as the stage winner — and retains the yellow jersey comfortably. He concedes the time bonuses to Pogačar and Evenepoel, who close the gaps slightly, but the Dane stays in command entering Stage 3 and the race's transition into France.

🇩🇪 Mattias Skjelmose Lidl-Trek
STAGE HUNTER P5 +0:03

Skjelmose initiates the stage-winning move on the final Montjuïc ascent, launching the attack that forces del Toro to respond. He is reeled in and then overpowered by the Mexican, finishing fifth at +3 seconds. The stage confirms Skjelmose's punch on the hilly finale, even if the win eludes him.

🇫🇷 Romain Grégoire Groupama-FDJ United
STAGE HUNTER P7 +0:03

Grégoire survives the Montjuïc selection and finishes seventh, just 3 seconds behind the stage winner. He moves to seventh in the GC at +0:44, confirming his ability to hold with the best on puncheur terrain. A promising return for the young Frenchman in only his second Tour.

🇫🇷 Lenny Martinez Bahrain Victorious
STAGE HUNTER P8 +0:03

Martinez finishes eighth in the lead group at +3 seconds, putting in a solid hilly-stage performance for Bahrain. He moves to ninth in the GC at +0:53 — a better-than-expected position for a team that posted a ninth-place TTT. The climb to Montjuïc suits his punch and he goes into Stage 3 with growing confidence.

🇬🇧 Tom Pidcock Pinarello-Q36.5 Pro Cycling Team
STAGE HUNTER P10 +0:03

Pidcock holds on through the Montjuïc circuit to finish tenth at +3 seconds — a performance that puts him tenth overall in the GC at +1:00. The British climber's ability to stay with the best at the Montjuïc finale bodes well for the mountain stages ahead.

🇳🇱 Mathieu van der Poel Alpecin-Premier Tech
HELPER —

Van der Poel is dropped on the final ascent of the Côte du Château de Montjuïc, leaving the lead group reduced to around 15 riders. His Tour focus on sprint and classic-style stage wins means this hilly circuit finale is not his preferred terrain, and he concedes time on the GC leaders.
