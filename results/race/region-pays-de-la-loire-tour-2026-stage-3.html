§ 01 · The stage

L'Hote attacks alone into Le Mans and rides the stage win into the overall.

After two flat sprint days the race turned on its hilly closing stages, where the general classification was finally made. On the stage to Sainte-Suzanne-et-Chammes, Antoine L'Hote (Decathlon CMA CGM) lit the finale, getting a small gap before a six-rider group — including Benjamin Thomas (Cofidis) — came across. The leaders watched one another on the final rise but held off the chasing peloton, and Alexander Kamp, winner of this race in 2023, took the reduced sprint ahead of Gabriele Bessega and L'Hote to claim the leader's jersey one day out.

The overall was decided on the run to Le Mans. Already aggressive the day before, L'Hote attacked clear inside the final 15 kilometres and committed everything to a solo move, resisting the return of the chase group all the way into the streets of Le Mans. He soloed across the line for the stage — only the second professional win of his career — ahead of Corbin Strong and Clément Venturini.

The time L'Hote gained flipped the GC: he took the overall by 12 seconds from Kamp, with Strong third at 18 seconds, Venturini fourth at 25 and Martin Marcellusi fifth at 31. (Note: in the actual 2026 race these were two separate stages — Kamp's hilly stage 3 and L'Hote's stage 4 into Le Mans — combined here to fit the calendar's single closing road stage.)

§ 02 · GC impact

L'Hote snatches the overall on the final day; Strong holds the podium.

Antoine L'Hote wins the general classification, 12 seconds clear of Alexander Kamp, with Corbin Strong third at 18 seconds, Clément Venturini fourth at 25 seconds and Martin Marcellusi fifth at 31 seconds. The overall changed hands twice in two days — Kamp taking it on the first hilly stage, L'Hote taking it back with his closing-stage solo — leaving a tightly stacked final podium decided entirely on the punchy finale.
