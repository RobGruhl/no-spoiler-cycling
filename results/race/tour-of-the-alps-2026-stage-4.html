§ 01 · The stage

Jasch solos to a surprise win as the GC stays frozen

The 167.8 km queen stage from Arco to Trento went to the breakaway. Lennart Jasch attacked clear of the move and soloed to the biggest win of his career in Trento, with Matteo Sobrero (+10s) and Federico Iacomoni (+11s) chasing home behind. The GC favourites watched each other up the road, the leaders' group arriving 20 seconds after Jasch with Florian Stork and Thymen Arensman among them. Jasch's points across the day's climbs also moved him toward the mountains classification, which he would go on to win. With nobody willing to blink among the GC men, the overall battle was deferred to the final stage.

§ 02 · GC impact

Four seconds covers the top three into the final day

Pellizzari retained the leader's jersey but the margins were razor-thin: he led Thymen Arensman and Egan Bernal by just four seconds, with Aleksandr Vlasov a further couple of seconds back and Mattia Gaffuri close behind. With six seconds covering the top four, the Trento–Bolzano finale loomed as a genuine showdown — exactly the scenario that suited a pure climber on home roads.
