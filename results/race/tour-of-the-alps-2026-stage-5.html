§ 01 · The stage

Pellizzari solos on Montoppio to seal the overall

The final stage opened at a relentless pace, with repeated attacks before a large, dangerous breakaway formed — Tom Pidcock, Stage 4 winner Lennart Jasch, Sam Oomen and Koen Bouwman among them. The move built over two minutes, briefly putting Juan Felipe Rodriguez into the virtual race lead and forcing Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe to defend Pellizzari's slender four-second cushion. On the 12.7 km climb to Nobls/Montoppio the break shattered and was reeled in, with Pidcock and Rodriguez among the last caught. Pellizzari then attacked from the reduced group, immediately separating from Arensman and Bernal. Behind, Michael Storer raised the pace; Bernal followed but Arensman couldn't respond and lost ground — the split that reshaped the podium. Pellizzari crested the climb alone and extended his lead on the descent to win the stage by around 30 seconds, converting his overnight lead into a clear overall victory.

§ 02 · GC impact

Pellizzari wins overall; Bernal second, Arensman third

Pellizzari sealed the maiden GC win of his career, finishing 40 seconds clear of Egan Bernal and 50 seconds ahead of Thymen Arensman in the final general classification. Michael Storer was fourth at +1:09 and Tudor's Mathys Rondel fifth at +1:45, with Aleksandr Vlasov sixth and Pidcock seventh. Pellizzari also took the youth classification; the points jersey went to Tommaso Dati and the mountains jersey to Lennart Jasch, with Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe winning the team classification.

Storylines from the stage

🇮🇹 Giulio Pellizzari Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER P1 stage winner + overall victory

The defining day of his young career. Defending a four-second lead, Pellizzari attacked from the GC group on the Montoppio climb, dropped Arensman and Bernal, and rode clear to win both the stage and the Tour of the Alps overall — the first GC title of his career, taken on Italian roads as the perfect Giro d'Italia tune-up. He extended his lead on the descent to finish around 30 seconds up, sealing the win by 40s over Bernal.

🇨🇴 Egan Bernal INEOS Grenadiers
LEADER P2 ~+0:30

Bernal was Pellizzari's most resilient challenger, the only GC rider able to follow Michael Storer's surge and limit the damage on Montoppio while Arensman cracked behind. Second on the stage and second overall at +40 — a strong marker on his ongoing comeback and a clear win in the INEOS internal battle with Arensman.

🇳🇱 Thymen Arensman INEOS Grenadiers
LEADER GC group +0:50 overall

Second overall starting the day, Arensman couldn't follow when Pellizzari attacked and Storer lifted the pace on Montoppio. He lost ground at the critical moment and slipped from second to third overall at +50, ceding the runner-up spot to teammate Bernal — a frustrating end to an otherwise consistent week.

🇦🇺 Michael Storer Tudor Pro Cycling Team
LEADER P3 +1:09 overall

The defending champion was a key animator on the final climb, raising the pace behind Pellizzari in a move that helped drop Arensman. He took third on the stage and finished fourth overall at +1:09 — Tudor's leading GC card in a race where the team also won the mountains classification through Lennart Jasch.
