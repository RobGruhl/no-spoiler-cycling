§ 01 · The stage

Wiebes wins then is disqualified; Balsamo inherits stage and pink

The 139 km opening stage from Cesenatico to Ravenna was always likely to end in a bunch sprint, and it did — but the result took hours to settle. An early three-rider Italian break (Valeria Curnis, Sharon Spimi, Sofia Arici) mopped up the early intermediate and KOM points before being reeled in.

The finale was nervous and crash-affected. A large peloton pile-up on a long straight brought down several riders; Movistar's Cat Ferguson stayed down and abandoned, ending her race on day one. The sprint trains reassembled in the closing kilometres, and Lorena Wiebes launched off a Barbara Guarischi lead-out to cross the line first, taking what looked like the win and the first maglia rosa, with Elisa Balsamo 2nd on the road and Lara Gillespie 3rd.

That evening the result changed. A post-stage technical check found Wiebes's bike to be roughly 20 grams under the UCI 6.8 kg minimum weight. She was disqualified from the stage and ejected from the race entirely. Balsamo was awarded the stage win and the first pink jersey of the 2026 Giro d'Italia Women, with the rest of the bunch promoted accordingly.

§ 02 · GC impact

Balsamo in pink after the Wiebes DQ

With Wiebes removed, Elisa Balsamo took the maglia rosa, with small finish-bonus gaps separating the front of the GC: Gillespie at +0:04, Consonni at +0:06, then a cluster (Baker, Kool, Zanetti, Verhulst-Wild) at +0:10. The GC favourites all finished safely in the bunch.
