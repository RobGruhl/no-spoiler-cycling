§ 01 · The race in 90 seconds

Race in progress — stages published as they finish

OPENINGBulgarian Grande Partenza: Magnier won the opening sprint into Burgas (Stage 1), Silva took the small-group sprint into Veliko Tarnovo (Stage 2) on a day defined by a descent crash that wiped out four UAE Team Emirates riders simultaneously (Vine, Soler, Adam Yates, Baroncini all withdrawn), Magnier doubled up with a second sprint win in Sofia (Stage 3).

UNFOLDSAfter the rest-day transfer to Italy: Narváez salvaged UAE's first stage win in Cosenza (Stage 4), Igor Arrieta won a rain-affected hilly day to Potenza (Stage 5), Ballerini took a cobbled sprint into Naples (Stage 6).

DECIDEDStage 7 — the Blockhaus 244 km queen stage — went to Vingegaard, 13 seconds clear of Gall, with Pellizzari 4th at +1:05 making the GC top-five on a home Italian mountain. The Giro's GC question is now mostly Visma's race to lose, with Decathlon's Gall the primary credible challenger.

FINALEStages 8 onwards will be added as they finish. Final overall GC will be written once the race is over.

§ 02 · Decisive moments

Where the race tilted

  1. 01 Stage 2 — Veliko Tarnovo descent
    UAE's Bulgarian disaster
    Mass crash on the rain-soaked descent into Veliko Tarnovo, ~21-23 km to go. Four UAE riders down at once. Vine (broken elbow + concussion), Soler (fractured pelvis), Adam Yates (delayed concussion), Baroncini all out within hours.
  2. 02 Stage 7 — Blockhaus summit
    Vingegaard's GC claim
    13.6 km final climb, 244 km stage, the longest of the Giro. Vingegaard attacked, only Gall could hold to within 13 seconds, the rest of the GC field lost a minute or more. Pellizzari held 4th.
§ 06 · Aftermath

Mid-race summary

The Giro's first week was defined by Bulgarian sprinting (Magnier × 2, Ballerini), UAE's Bulgarian descent disaster (four riders out from a single crash), Italian climbing rebellion (Arrieta the surprise of Stage 5, Pellizzari emerging across multiple stages), and then Vingegaard claiming the GC with a Blockhaus win that confirmed the Paris-Nice form line. The final two weeks will decide whether Gall can press the gap closer, whether Pellizzari can climb onto the podium, and whether anyone else still has a card to play.
