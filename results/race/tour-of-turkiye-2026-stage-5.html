§ 01 · The stage

Van Uden wins the long coastal stage to Kemer

At 180.7 km, the Patara to Kemer stage was the longest of the 2026 race, hugging the Mediterranean coast on a rolling profile that ultimately favoured the fast men. The Dutch sprinter Casper van Uden (Team Picnic PostNL) timed the finale best to take the win ahead of Marcin Budziński (MBH Bank) and Nikita Tsvetkov (Bardiani CSF 7 Saber). The GC group again came in together with no changes among the overall contenders, who were saving themselves for the decisive queen stage to Feslikan the following day.

§ 02 · GC impact

GC unchanged on the eve of the queen stage

Iván Sosa kept the turquoise jersey through another sprint day, his slim advantage over Sebastian Berwick intact heading into the queen stage. The mountains classification still lay with Sosa and the points jersey with Tom Crabbe. Everything was poised for the summit finish at Feslikan, the one climb hard enough to settle the overall.
