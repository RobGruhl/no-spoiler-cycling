§ 01 · The stage

Evenepoel wins the race of truth in Alginet

The 17 km individual time trial between Carlet and Alginet was always going to reshuffle the general classification, and Remco Evenepoel — one of the best time-triallists in the world — delivered as expected. He stopped the clock at 20:12, fastest on the day by a clear margin. His Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe team-mate Aleksandr Vlasov took second at eight seconds, with Lidl - Trek's Mathias Vacek third at sixteen. The ride moved Evenepoel into a commanding position on GC, though Biniam Girmay's earlier bonuses meant the leader's jersey was not yet settled.

§ 02 · GC impact

Evenepoel surges up the GC, Girmay clings to the lead

Evenepoel's time-trial win vaulted him to the top of the GC contenders, gapping rivals Almeida, Pellizzari and Tiberi who all conceded time against the clock. Biniam Girmay retained the overall lead on the strength of his Stage 1 bonus seconds, but the writing was on the wall for the summit-style finish to come on the queen stage.
