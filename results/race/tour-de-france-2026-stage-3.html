§ 01 · The stage

Pogacar pockets yellow on the first Pyrenean day as del Toro's selfless lead-out ignites the Les Angles summit.

Stage 3 covers 195.9 km from Granollers over three Pyrenean climbs — the Col de Toses, the Col du Calvaire, and the final ramp into the ski resort of Les Angles — for the race's first genuine summit finish. With more than an hour of skirmishing, a 19-rider breakaway finally forms including Egan Bernal, Mads Pedersen, Magnus Cort, Luke Plapp, Alex Baudin, Nicolas Prodhomme, and others. Bernal suffers a puncture on the Col de Toses descent and loses contact, ending a promising day for the former Tour winner.

The break's advantage swells to over three minutes before UAE Emirates, led by Florian Vermeersch, starts a controlled reduction. On the Col de Toses, Raúl García Pierna escapes solo; Vlad Van Mechelen and Mathéo Vercher bridge across partway up before Alex Baudin, George Bennett, and Prodhomme join on the upper slopes. Baudin crests the Col de Toses summit first, claiming the polka-dot jersey from Alex Molenaar. On the Col du Calvaire, Baudin attacks again and Prodhomme bridges; at that summit the pair still hold 43 seconds on the now-reduced peloton.

On the descent, Prodhomme sits up and rejoins the main group before the new King of the Mountains is caught by the peloton with 11 km remaining on the final Les Angles ramp. On the 1.7 km summit finish, Isaac del Toro delivers a prolonged lead-out for Pogacar that cracks any lingering attacks. Inside the final 200 metres, Pogacar unleashes his sprint and immediately opens a gap nobody can close. He crosses the line 2 seconds ahead of Vingegaard — Richard Carapaz and Paul Seixas finish in the same time as Vingegaard, with a second group of Tobias Halland Johannessen, Lennert Van Eetvelt, Florian Lipowitz, Remco Evenepoel, Isaac del Toro, and Juan Ayuso arriving 4 seconds back.

With 10 seconds of stage bonus (versus 6 for Vingegaard), Pogacar erases exactly the 6-second GC gap that separated them since Stage 1. The two rivals are level on aggregate time entering Stage 4 — Pogacar leads by virtue of the stage victory and dons yellow for the first time in this Tour.

§ 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇸🇮 Tadej Pogacar UAE Team Emirates XRG
LEADER P1

Pogacar times his effort perfectly on Les Angles, conserving energy while del Toro sets a punishing lead-out before uncorking a sprint in the last 200 metres that none of the GC group can answer. The 2-second margin over Vingegaard, combined with a 4-second bonus differential, cancels out the 6-second GC deficit from Stage 1 exactly — Pogacar takes the yellow jersey on stage-win countback. His stage win signals that UAE will dictate rather than follow through the Pyrenees.

🇩🇪 Jonas Vingegaard Team Visma | Lease a Bike
LEADER P2 +0:02

Vingegaard matches Pogacar stride for stride over the Col du Calvaire and the Les Angles ramp but is pipped in the sprint, conceding 2 seconds in stage time and 4 seconds in bonus differential — precisely the margin that transfers yellow. He exits Stage 3 level on aggregate time with Pogacar, second only on countback, confirming the Tour remains a two-man contest. His Visma squad kept rivals at bay through the descent to the final climb.

🏳️ Richard Carapaz EF Education - EasyPost
STAGE HUNTER P3 +0:02

Carapaz delivers EF Education-EasyPost's best result of this Tour, finishing third on the Les Angles summit at the same time as Vingegaard. The Ecuadorian entered Stage 3 well outside the top 10 GC but climbs ferociously on the final ramp, earning the 4-second bonus and moving to twelfth overall at +1:45. His teammate Baudin also excelled in the breakaway — a standout day for the EF squad in the Pyrenees.

🇧🇪 Remco Evenepoel Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER P8 +0:04

Evenepoel holds with the second GC group through the Les Angles summit, finishing eighth at +0:04. He moves to third overall at +0:23 — a controlled but watchful performance. Red Bull-Bora kept their two GC leaders Evenepoel and Lipowitz both in the main group, limiting their losses to a handful of seconds on the day's first genuine mountain test.

🏳️ Florian Lipowitz Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
LEADER P7 +0:04

Lipowitz finishes seventh in the second GC group at +0:04, holding seventh overall at +0:53. Quiet and efficient — he sits in the wheels through the Col du Calvaire and survives the Les Angles finish comfortably. With Evenepoel also in the group, Red Bull-Bora keeps two GC leaders well placed through the first big mountain day.

🇲🇽 Isaac del Toro UAE Team Emirates XRG
DOMESTIQUE P9 +0:04

After winning Stage 2, del Toro switches entirely to a domestique role for Stage 3, delivering a sustained lead-out on the final Les Angles ramp that sets the conditions for Pogacar's winning sprint. He finishes ninth at +0:04 — still in the main GC group — and holds fourth overall at +0:24. The 22-year-old's willingness to ride for a teammate immediately after his first Tour stage win underlines UAE's collective depth and tactical discipline.

🇩🇪 Mattias Skjelmose Lidl-Trek
LEADER P11 +0:10

Skjelmose finishes 11th at +0:10, holding on closer to the summit group than feared but still unable to follow Pogacar's final surge. He sits 15th in GC at +1:50 after the stage — a gap that leaves him well outside the top-10 battle. On pure summit finishes the Dane continues to lag behind the elite GC climbers.

🇫🇷 Lenny Martinez Bahrain Victorious
LEADER P13 +0:12

Martinez finishes 13th at +0:12, staying in contact with the top GC group through the summit. He sits 8th overall at +1:09 after the stage — a manageable gap for a rider with strong mountain instincts. He remains inside the top 10 and within range of a potential top-5 finish in Paris.

🇫🇷 Romain Grégoire Groupama-FDJ United
STAGE HUNTER P75 +20:18

A brutal day for Grégoire. The young Frenchman, promising in GC terms entering the Pyrenees, is dropped far from the summit and finishes 75th at +20:18 — well over 20 minutes adrift of the winner. The first true summit finish exposes the gap between his puncheur talents and the pure climbers leading the race. His GC ambitions are effectively ended by Stage 3.

🇳🇱 Thymen Arensman Netcompany-INEOS Cycling Team
DOMESTIQUE P19 +0:27

Arensman finishes 19th at +0:27 — a solid result from a Netcompany-INEOS domestique. He sits 16th in GC at +2:19 after the stage, slightly behind the top GC group but within the same peloton grouping. His climbing abilities keep him in the picture even if his primary job is protecting GC teammates.

🇳🇱 Mathieu van der Poel Alpecin-Premier Tech
STAGE HUNTER P125 +24:17

Stage 3's Pyrenean climbing is not van der Poel's terrain. He finishes 125th at +24:17 — deep in the gruppetto — which is entirely expected and accepted. Not a concern for his Tour ambitions; he remains Alpecin-Premier Tech's focal point for flatter stages and sprints ahead.

🇬🇧 Tom Pidcock Pinarello-Q36.5 Pro Cycling Team
STAGE HUNTER P16 +0:18

Pidcock finishes 16th at +0:18, limiting his losses on a pure mountain stage. He sits 11th in GC at +1:22 after the stage — staying within range of the top 10 and keeping his options open for more suitable terrain ahead.

🇫🇷 Alex Baudin EF Education - EasyPost
STAGE HUNTER P47 +4:08

Baudin is the star of Stage 3's breakaway. The young Frenchman crests the Col de Toses summit first, claiming the polka-dot jersey and becoming the new King of the Mountains ahead of Alex Molenaar. He attacks again on the Col du Calvaire, extending his KOM lead at that summit. Only caught 11 km from the Les Angles finish as UAE's peloton reduces the gap to seconds — Baudin delivers one of the most aggressive breakaway performances of the early Tour, and earns his team a 3rd-place stage result via Carapaz.
