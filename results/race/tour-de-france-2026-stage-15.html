Stage 15: Champagnole → Plateau de Solaison
Tour de France 2026

Remco Evenepoel claims his first major mountain stage win at the Tour de France, neutralising two attacks from Isaac del Toro before sprinting to victory on the Plateau de Solaison, with Tadej Pogacar crossing in the same time. Jonas Vingegaard crashed at 20 kilometres to go and abandoned the race with a suspected fractured collarbone. Pogacar retains the yellow jersey; Evenepoel rises to second overall.

§ 01 · The stage

Evenepoel sprints to first Tour mountain stage win as Vingegaard abandons after crash.

Stage 15's 183.9 kilometres from Champagnole to the Plateau de Solaison was marked by drama from the opening kilometres. Overnight, Jonas Vingegaard underwent a doping test at 2am and Tadej Pogacar at 5am — Pogacar would later note he had slept just four hours. Team Visma | Lease a Bike arrived at the start with clear tactical intent: push Pogacar's fatigue and test the yellow jersey early.

After an intermediate sprint in which Jasper Philipsen clawed five points back from Mads Pedersen, a 24-rider breakaway formed featuring Tom Pidcock (with teammate Brent van Moer), Egan Bernal, Quinn Simmons, Ilan van Wilder, Ben Healy, Mauro Schmid, Ewen Costiou, Adam Yates, Einer Rubio, Thymen Arensman, Tobias Halland Johannessen and others. Visma kept the gap at no more than two minutes rather than letting the break go. On the Col de la Croisette, Pidcock drilled the pace and shattered the breakaway, with Simmons escaping to crest five seconds ahead of Pidcock, Yannis Voisard, Yates, and Rubio. Costiou and Schmid rejoined the leaders on the Côte du Mont; the Swiss rider then went clear alone on the descent that followed.

With 20 kilometres to the summit finish, the stage — and the race — changed irreversibly. Jonas Vingegaard and Isaac del Toro were caught in the same crash on a technical section while Visma was setting a high pace in the peloton. Del Toro remounted and rejoined the GC group; Vingegaard, immediately in pain, did not — his withdrawal confirmed shortly afterward with a suspected fractured collarbone. Visma, without their leader, abandoned the front of the race.

Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe continued chasing to set up Evenepoel on the start of the Plateau de Solaison's 11-kilometre ascent. Pogacar then assumed control of the GC group, lifting the pace until only del Toro and Evenepoel could follow. The trio bridged to the remnants of the breakaway (Simmons leading the charge) with 5.5 kilometres remaining, sweeping through the exhausted escapees. Inside the final kilometre, del Toro attacked twice; both times Evenepoel accelerated onto his wheel and neutralised the move. After the second attack was smothered, del Toro led out the sprint — but Evenepoel came around him with decisive power. Pogacar, whether from fatigue after the overnight doping test or a tactical choice, did not contest; del Toro crossed six seconds down.

§ 04 · Rider-by-rider · tracked riders

Storylines from the stage

🇧🇪 Remco Evenepoel Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
STAGE HUNTER P1

A landmark win for Evenepoel: his first major mountain stage victory at the Tour de France. Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe set the early tempo after Vingegaard's crash, and Evenepoel showed his tactical patience throughout the final climb, letting Pogacar dictate the pace while staying glued to his wheel. When del Toro attacked inside the final kilometre — twice — Evenepoel covered each move immediately and never allowed a gap to open. After the second attack was neutralised, del Toro led out the sprint but Evenepoel came around him with decisive power; Pogacar did not contest. The win also moved him from third to second in the overall classification.

🇸🇮 Tadej Pogacar UAE Team Emirates - XRG
LEADER P2 s.t.

Pogacar controlled the stage from the GC group, accelerating on the Plateau de Solaison to shred all rivals except del Toro and Evenepoel. After the three bridged to the breakaway, Pogacar appeared to conserve energy in the final kilometre — he covered neither of del Toro's attacks nor contested Evenepoel's sprint, settling for second. Whether the overnight doping test and four hours of sleep affected his final kick is debatable; he remains firmly in yellow with a 5:00 advantage over Evenepoel.

🇲🇽 Isaac del Toro UAE Team Emirates - XRG
STAGE HUNTER INCIDENT +0:06

Del Toro's stage was defined by two crashes and two attacks. He first hit the deck alongside Vingegaard with 20 kilometres to go — remounting and rejoining the GC group quickly. He then followed Pogacar's acceleration on the final climb as the only UAE rider (along with Evenepoel) who could stay with the yellow jersey. In the final kilometre he attacked twice in an attempt to shed Evenepoel; both were neutralised. He crossed six seconds down, still delivering a strong result and climbing from seventh to third in the overall classification.

⚠ Crashed at 164km to go in same incident as Vingegaard; remounted and finished
🇩🇪 Jonas Vingegaard Team Visma | Lease a Bike
LEADER INCIDENT

Vingegaard's Tour ended on Stage 15. Visma had been pushing a high tempo to test Pogacar's fatigue following his overnight doping test. With 20 kilometres remaining, Vingegaard hit the deck in the same incident as del Toro on a technical section of the descent. He immediately showed signs of serious injury and withdrew shortly after, confirmed with a suspected fractured collarbone. At the point of his crash he was second overall at +4:30 — the race's closest rival to Pogacar.

⚠ Abandoned Tour de France after crash with 20km to go; suspected fractured collarbone
🇫🇷 Paul Seixas Decathlon CMA CGM Team
STAGE HUNTER P4 +0:58

Seixas finished fourth for the second consecutive stage, arriving nearly a minute behind the podium trio. He was unable to follow when Pogacar ramped the pace on the Plateau de Solaison, chasing from behind along with Lipowitz. His overall position stays at fourth (+6:23), though the gap to del Toro in third has grown slightly.

🏳️ Florian Lipowitz Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe
STAGE HUNTER P5 s.t.

Lipowitz came in fifth at the same time as Seixas, roughly a minute down on the stage winner. He rises one place in the GC to fifth overall (+6:48) with Vingegaard's abandon, and remains the second Red Bull - BORA - hansgrohe rider in the top five alongside stage winner Evenepoel.

🇫🇷 Lenny Martinez Bahrain Victorious
STAGE HUNTER P6 +1:57

Martinez finished sixth, nearly two minutes down on the winner, but moved up to eighth in the overall classification (+10:28) as Vingegaard's abandon reshuffled the standings. A solid day for the young French climber on a demanding Alpine summit finish.

🇩🇪 Mattias Skjelmose Lidl-Trek
STAGE HUNTER P7 s.t.

Skjelmose came in seventh at the same time as Martinez, finishing at the same point as several GC rivals who had been dropped from the top trio. He moves to seventh overall (+9:38) with Vingegaard's withdrawal.

🏳️ Juan Ayuso Lidl-Trek
STAGE HUNTER P8 +2:00

Ayuso finished eighth, two minutes down on the stage. He moves to sixth in the overall classification (+7:25) after Vingegaard's abandon rearranged the standings.

🏳️ Adam Yates UAE Team Emirates - XRG
HELPER P9 s.t.

Yates was one of the UAE riders in the 24-man breakaway, supporting the team's presence up the road while Pogacar was in the peloton. He was eventually absorbed by the GC group. Ninth place at the same time as Ayuso.

🏳️ Tom Pidcock Pinarello Q36.5 Pro Cycling Team
STAGE HUNTER —

Pidcock was the driving force of the breakaway, setting the tempo on the Col de la Croisette and briefly leading the race with around 9km to go on the Plateau de Solaison. However, the GC trio's pace on the final climb proved too fast and Pidcock was overtaken. He finished outside the top 10, losing nearly three minutes on the stage, and slips from ninth to ninth in the GC but at a widened gap of +10:56.
