Bretagne Classic–Ouest France

The 95th Bretagne Classic — Ouest-France is a one-day UCI WorldTour race held on Sunday August 30, 2026, starting and finishing in Plouay (Morbihan), Brittany. The course is the classic Plouay circuit: rolling Breton terrain with a punchy 14km finishing loop containing the Côte de Restavy and the closing climb into the village. The race traditionally favours puncheurs and Classics specialists able to handle multiple short climbs over a long day. It is the final WorldTour one-day Classic before the late-summer Spanish Grand Tour build-up.

§ 05 — Storylines

Narratives to watch

  • Plouay's 95th edition: Organised continuously since 1931 by the Comité des Fêtes (now Plouay Cyclisme Organisation), the race is one of the oldest French one-day classics on the calendar.
  • Vuelta crossroads: Held the same week the Vuelta a España begins, the race is the final hit-out for Classics riders ahead of the autumn block — start list is a mix of WT regulars and form-seekers.
  • Plouay circuit: Multiple laps of the 14km finishing circuit (Côte de Restavy, closing climb into Plouay) shape the closing tactics — break-or-be-broken on the late laps.
  • Breton crowd: One of the most fervently supported one-day races in France — the local Plouay community treats it as a regional festival.
§ 04 — Historical Context

Form book & lore

First held in 1931 as the Grand Prix de Plouay, the race was renamed Bretagne Classic — Ouest-France in 2015 and joined the UCI WorldTour the same year. Sponsored by the Ouest-France newspaper, the event is the largest cycling fixture in Brittany and a regional cultural institution.

§ 07 — Viewing Notes

When to tune in

Decisive moves typically come on the final two laps of the Plouay circuit. Reduced-bunch finishes are common; pure bunch sprints are rare given the punchy circuit terrain.
