Vuelta Asturias

Vuelta a Asturias (Vuelta Ciclista a Asturias Julio Álvarez Mendo) is a three-stage Spanish UCI 2.1 race held across April 23-25, 2026 in the Principality of Asturias. Known for short but brutal stages that crisscross the Cantabrian Mountains, it features the famous Alto del Naranco summit finish in Oviedo. Despite its modest length, the race is genuinely mountainous and regularly produces selective GC battles among Spanish Pro Team and invited WorldTour squads.

§ 05 — Historical Context

Form book & lore

Vuelta a Asturias dates back to 1925 and is one of Spain's oldest stage races. Past winners include Luis Herrera, Alberto Contador, Nairo Quintana and other climbing legends. The Alto del Naranco summit finish is a symbol of Asturian cycling.

§ 06 — Viewing Notes

When to tune in

Short stages, big climbs - every kilometre matters. Watch for GC moves on the final two climbs of each stage. Coverage typically on RTVE Spain and TeleDeporte, with highlights on YouTube.
