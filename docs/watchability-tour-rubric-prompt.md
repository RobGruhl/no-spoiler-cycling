# Aggregate TOUR watchability — one fire rating (1–5) for a whole stage race

Rate how worth-following the ENTIRE multi-day race was, for a spoiler-free fan
deciding whether to follow the tour. This is NOT a naive average of the stages —
it's a holistic judgement of the event as a spectacle.

You are given: the race overview JSON (final GC, jerseys, narrative, aftermath) and,
for each stage, its already-judged per-stage fire (1–5) + the stage results JSON
(read the tldr/narrative as needed).

Rate UP for:
- A genuine GC battle that stayed alive deep into the race (lead changes, small gaps,
  decided on the final mountain/TT) — sustained suspense across days
- Several thrilling stages (multiple 4s/5s), aggressive racing, big upsets, chaos
  (echelons, weather, crashes reshaping GC), a beloved/emotional overall win
- A breakthrough or unexpected overall winner

Rate DOWN for:
- A GC settled early and defended without drama (a procession to the finish)
- Mostly routine bunch-sprint stages with one decisive mountain day and no suspense
- A dominant favourite controlling throughout exactly as expected

SCALE (be discerning; most solid tours are 3; reserve 5 for an all-time-classic
edition; do not inflate for prestige — a dull Grand Tour can be a 3, a thrilling
minor stage race a 5):
  5 = an all-time classic edition — follow every day
  4 = a really good tour, gripping GC or many great stages
  3 = solid, worth following the key days
  2 = mostly forgettable; catch the highlights
  1 = a procession; skip to the summary
  0 = abandoned/void

Your NUMBER is shown publicly; your rationale is NOT.
