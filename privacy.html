Privacy

What we store
and what we don't

Last updated 2026-07-04

No Spoiler Cycling is a static website. There are no user accounts, no login, and no server that records who you are. This page explains, in plain language, the small amount of data the site keeps in your own browser and the third parties you may reach by following links.

What the site stores on your device

To make the site usable, we save a few preferences locally in your browser (using cookies and localStorage). This information stays on your device — it is not sent to us or to anyone else.

  • Filter and display preferences — the calendar filters you pick (discipline, region, rating, and similar) so the page looks the way you left it.
  • The spoiler gate — results pages contain spoilers, so your choice to reveal or keep hiding results is remembered on your device so you are not re-prompted every visit.

You can clear this at any time by clearing your browser's site data for this domain. Doing so simply resets the site to its defaults.

What we do not do

  • No accounts. We never ask you to sign up or log in.
  • No analytics. We do not currently run any analytics or tracking scripts, and we do not build a profile of you.
  • No advertising. There are currently no ads and no ad networks on the site.
  • No selling of data. There is no personal data collected to sell.

If any of this changes in the future, we will update this page before turning such a feature on.

Fonts and external requests

The site uses your device's built-in system fonts and does not load fonts or stylesheets from third-party services such as Google Fonts. That means normal page views do not make requests to outside font or CDN providers.

Links to other sites

The site links out to third parties — for example broadcasters, race organizers, and YouTube videos. When you follow one of those links, or when an embedded video loads, that third party handles your visit under its own privacy policy, which we do not control. Please review their policies for details on what they collect.

Questions

Questions about privacy? Open an issue on the project's GitHub issues page. Please do not include sensitive personal information in a public issue.
